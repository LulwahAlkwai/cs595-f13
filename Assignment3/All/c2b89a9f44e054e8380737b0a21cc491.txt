<h3 style="color: #900; font-size: 28px; clear: left;">Privacy is Non-negotiable: Tell congress to oppose CISPA 2.0</h3> 


<div><strong>CISPA is back.&nbsp;</strong></div>
<div><span><br /></span></div>
<div><span>Last year, our collective efforts helped put a stop to one of the gravest threats to online privacy and internet freedom we have ever seen -- The Cyber Intelligence Sharing and Protection Act. </span></div>
<div><span><br /></span></div>
<div><span>The bill grants companies unprecedented power to obtain users' personal information (such as from private communications) and to disclose that data to the government without a warrant -- including sending data to the National Security Agency.&nbsp;</span></div>
<div><span><br /></span></div>
<div><strong><span>This week, CISPA was reintroduced in the House of Representatives.</span></strong><span>&nbsp;</span></div>
<div><span><br /></span></div>
<div><span>And the new bill has the very same dangerous problems as last year's version. CISPA 2.0 would </span>grant immunity to private companies who share your data with other companies, private agencies, and the government.&nbsp;</div>
<div>&nbsp;</div>
<div><span>And CISPA permits the use of your personal data for reasons completely unrelated to cybersecurity.&nbsp;</span></div>
<div><span><br /></span></div>
<div><strong>Now, as before, we cannot sacrifice our hard-won liberties and privacy rights in the pursuit of a misguided and overbroad conception of "security."&nbsp;</strong></div>
<div><strong><br /></strong></div>
<div>We need your help.</div>
