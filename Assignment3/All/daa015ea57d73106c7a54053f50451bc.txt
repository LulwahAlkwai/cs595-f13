	<h2 id="post-4038">No longer embargoed: The 10 Embargo Watch embargo&nbsp;guidelines</h2>
			<p class="comments"><a href="http://embargowatch.wordpress.com/2013/09/11/no-longer-embargoed-the-10-embargo-watch-embargo-guidelines/#comments">leave a comment &raquo;</a></p>
	
	<div class="main">
		<div id="attachment_4039" class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 310px"><a href="http://embargowatch.files.wordpress.com/2013/09/ten-commandments.jpg"><img class="size-medium wp-image-4039" alt="Photo by David Berkowitz http://www.flickr.com/photos/davidberkowitz/" src="http://embargowatch.files.wordpress.com/2013/09/ten-commandments.jpg?w=300&#038;h=225" width="300" height="225" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">Photo by David Berkowitz <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/davidberkowitz/" rel="nofollow">http://www.flickr.com/photos/davidberkowitz/</a></p></div>
<p>Soon after I launched Embargo Watch in early 2010, various reporters and public affairs officers have asked me to gather the various &#8220;rules&#8221; I&#8217;ve suggested for embargoes into one place, using vignettes that illustrate how they&#8217;ve been broken. That always felt like a good idea, but not one I ever found time for.</p>
<p>Well, I finally got around to it, thanks to a request from my friend <a href="https://twitter.com/fabioturone">Fabio Turone</a>, the managing editor of <em>Epidemiology, Biostatistics, and Public Health</em>. <span id="more-4038"></span>You can now read my list of <a href="http://ebph.it/article/view/9043/8190">10 guidelines for using embargoes</a>, titled &#8220;If you must use embargoes, here&#8217;s how to do it right.&#8221; It&#8217;s everything from not putting an embargo on freely available information, to how much time to give reporters, and more.</p>
<p>Feedback is, as always, encouraged.</p>
