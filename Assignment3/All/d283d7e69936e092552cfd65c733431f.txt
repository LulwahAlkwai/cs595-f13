		<h1 class="cnnBlogContentTitle"><a href="http://news.blogs.cnn.com/2013/09/16/several-injured-at-washington-d-c-navy-yard-after-shots-fired/" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link:Live blog: 12, plus suspect, dead after Navy Yard shooting">Live blog: 12, plus suspect, dead after Navy Yard shooting</a></h1>		<div class="cnnBlogContentPost">
			<p class="cnn_first">Shots were <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2013/09/16/us/dc-navy-yard-gunshots/index.html">fired Monday at a Washington Navy Yard building</a>, killing at least 12 people and injuring 14 others, according to local officials and the Navy.</p>
<p>Also killed was a suspect, Aaron Alexis, a 34-year-old military information-technology contractor and former full-time Navy reservist who lived in Texas, the FBI said. One other gunman may be on the loose, police said.</p>
<p>The incident began about 8:20 a.m. ET when several shots were fired inside the Naval Sea Systems Command headquarters in the southeast portion of the capital. Developments below:</p>
<p><strong>[Updated at 10:47 p.m. ET]</strong> Police released the names and ages of seven of the 12 people killed in the shooting. None of the seven was military personnel:</p>
<p>&#8211; Michael Arnold, 59<br />
&#8211; Sylvia Frasier, 53<br />
&#8211; Kathy Gaarde, 62<br />
&#8211; John Roger Johnson, 73<br />
&#8211; Frank Kohler, 50<br />
&#8211; Kenneth Bernard Proctor, 46<br />
&#8211; Vishnu Pandit, 61.</p>
<p><strong>[Updated at 10:40 p.m. ET]</strong> Alexis had access to the yard because of his contracting work, and he used a valid pass to enter the yard, said Valerie Parlave, assistant director in charge of the Washington FBI field office.</p>
<p><strong>[Updated at 10:39 p.m. ET]</strong> Besides the 13 people who were killed, eight people were injured in Monday morning&#039;s shooting, Washington Mayor Vincent Gray told reporters Monday night. Three of those were injured by gunfire, and the others had other types of injuries, such as contusions and chest pain.</p>
<p>Earlier Monday night, Navy Vice Adm. William D. French said 14 people were injured. The 13 dead include suspect Aaron Alexis.</p>
<p><strong>[Updated at 10:38 p.m. ET]</strong> Washington police are confident that only one person was involved in Monday morning&#039;s shooting, and they are lifting a shelter-in-place order for residents who live nearby, Police Chief Cathy Lanier said Monday night. Authorities have said suspect Aaron Alexis, 34, was killed after an encounter with security.</p>
<p><span id="more-161026"></span></p>
<p><strong>[Updated at 9:37 p.m. ET]</strong> One of the weapons that Alexis is accused of using was purchased recently at a store in northern Virginia, an FBI source with firsthand knowledge of the investigation told CNN&#039;s Pamela Brown.</p>
<p><strong>[Updated at 8:52 p.m. ET]</strong> Alexis worked with The Experts, a subcontractor working on Hewlett Packard&#039;s contracts with the military, according to The Experts&#039; chief executive officer, Thomas Hoshko.</p>
<p>Alexis received secret-level security clearance through the Defense Department twice in roughly the past year: Once for an assignment with The Experts to work on an HP contract in Japan refreshing computer systems late last year and early this year, and again in July for the Navy Yard assignment, Hoshko told CNN&#039;s Drew Griffin.</p>
<p><strong>[Updated at 8:43 p.m. ET]</strong> The Washington police officer who had surgery after being shot in his legs is &#034;alert, he&#039;s talkative, and he&#039;s doing very well,&#034; said Janis Orlowski, the chief medical officer at Medstar Washington Hospital Center.</p>
<p>Earlier, CNN&#039;s Chris Lawrence reported that doctors will begin to determine on Tuesday whether he will be able to keep the legs.</p>
<p><strong>[Updated at 8:27 p.m. ET]</strong> Besides the 13 people who were killed, 14 people were injured in Monday morning&#039;s shooting at the Washington Navy Yard, Navy Vice Adm. William D. French told reporters Monday night. The 13 dead include suspect Aaron Alexis.</p>
<p><strong>[Updated at 8:26 p.m. ET]</strong> About 2,000 people have yet to be released from the Washington Navy Yard after Monday morning&#039;s shooting there, though officials hope to let them go in the next three hours, Navy Vice Admiral William D. French told reporters. The military is working with the FBI to process people on the base and release them, and officials are about halfway through the process, French said.</p>
<p><strong>[Updated at 8:05 p.m. ET]</strong> Adding to what we reported earlier about Alexis&#039; movements (see 7:11 p.m. post) - He entered the facility legally with a valid ID card, a federal law enforcement official told CNN&#039;s Susan Candiotti.</p>
<p>Alexis used a legally purchased AR-15 gun, according to the official.</p>
<p><strong>[Updated at 8:03 p.m. ET]</strong> In Seattle, police say they arrested Alexis in 2004 for shooting out the tires of another man&#039;s vehicle in what Alexis later told detectives was an anger-fueled &#034;blackout.&#034;</p>
<p><strong>[Updated at 7:53 p.m. ET]</strong> The wounded Washington police officer who was in surgery today at Medstar Washington Hospital Center was shot multiple times in the legs, and doctors will begin to determine on Tuesday whether he will be able to keep the legs, CNN&#039;s Chris Lawrence reported.</p>
<p><strong>[Updated at 7:42 p.m. ET]</strong> The names of those killed won&#039;t be released until 24 hours after the last relative on the entire list is notified of the deaths, a Pentagon official told CNN&#039;s Barbara Starr. This is the same way the Defense Department treats a mass casualty incident in a warzone.</p>
<p><strong>[Updated at 7:11 p.m. ET]</strong> Before the shooting, Alexis drove into the naval yard with his military contractor identification card, a U.S. law enforcement official told CNN&#039;s Evan Perez.</p>
<p>Alexis, armed with three guns, walked into Building 197, made his way to an overlook above an atrium, and started shooting, the official said.</p>
<p><strong>[Updated at 6:54 p.m. ET]</strong> Multiple law enforcement sources say that only one weapon was recovered from the scene of the shooting, CNN&#039;s Evan Perez and Pamela Brown report. The sources say the weapon was a &#034;long gun.&#034;</p>
<p><strong>[Updated at 6:47 p.m. ET]</strong> Michael Ritrovato, a friend and former roommate of Alexis, told CNN that Alexis was <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2013/09/16/us/navy-yard-suspects/index.html" target="_blank">frustrated with the company that contracted him to work for the Navy</a>.</p>
<p>Alexis claimed he wasn&#039;t paid properly by the company after returning from a months-long assignment to Japan last year, Ritrovato said.</p>
<p>It was unclear whether the dispute was over salary or expenses. Alexis just felt the company owed him money and had not paid him, Ritrovato said.</p>
<p><strong>[Updated at 6:44 p.m. ET]</strong> One of Alexis&#039; friends, Nutpisit Suthamtewakul, told CNN&#039;s Ed Lavandera that <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2013/09/16/us/navy-yard-suspects/index.html" target="_blank">he had been Alexis&#039; roommate for three years</a> near Fort Worth, Texas, ending a few months ago when Suthamtewakul got married.</p>
<p>Alexis befriended Suthamtewakul four years ago after he emigrated from Thailand, and taught Suthamtewakul about American culture, Suthamtewakul told CNN. Alexis, he said, was fluent in Thai and attended a Buddhist temple.</p>
<p>When Suthamtewakul opened the Happy Bowl Thai Restaurant, Alexis would occasionally help out, waiting tables, he said.</p>
<p>Suthamtewakul reeled at the news that Alexis was believed to be a gunman in the rampage at the Navy Yard.</p>
<p>&#034;I can&#039;t believe he did this,&#034; he said. &#034;He never showed any sign of violence.&#034;</p>

<p><strong>[Updated at 1:33 p.m. ET]</strong> Some background on the Navy Yard:</p>
<p>The 3,000-employee facility has a an annual budget of almost $30 billion.</p>
<p>It is the Navy&#039;s oldest land establishment, created by act of Congress in 1799, according to the Naval History and Heritage Command. Located along the Anacostia River, it was initially intended as a shipbuilding facility and once serviced the Navy&#039;s most famous vessels, including the USS Constitution.</p>
<p>But after it was burned during the War of 1812, the Navy Yard was transformed into a center for ordnance and technological development. The facility was the world&#039;s largest ordnance plant during World War II, but its military role diminished during the Cold War. Today, it includes the headquarters for Naval District Washington and a naval museum.</p>

		window.CNNVIDEOS[ frame ] = window.frames[frame];
		window.frames[frame].postMessage(JSON.stringify({
				action: 'play',
				name: frame,

				pageUrl:document.location.href,

	function broker(e){

			var data=(typeof e.data === "object")?e.data:JSON.parse(e.data);
			if(data.action){
				if(data.action==='play'){
					//pause other players
					pause(data.name);
				}
			}
		}catch(err){

		}
	}
	function pause(from){
		var videoFrames=document.querySelectorAll('iframe.videoPlayerFrame');
		for(var i=0,l=videoFrames.length;i<l;i++){
			var frame=videoFrames[i];
			if(frame.name !== from){
				window.frames[frame.name].postMessage({"action": "pause"},window.framePath);
			}
		}
	}

	if(window.attachEvent){
		window.attachEvent("onmessage", broker);
	}else{
		window.addEventListener('message', broker, false);
