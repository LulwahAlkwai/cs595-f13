<h2>In My Hands</h2>
<p>Appleâ€™s new iOS 7 looks right at home on the iPhone 5C (possibly even more so than the iPhone 5s), as its colorful exterior appears to almost exactly match the operating system's primary-color palette.</p>
<p>Slightly larger than the iPhone 5s (and 5), the iPhone 5C felt cool, comfortable and sturdy in my hand. The one-piece plastic body is shiny and smooth to the touch. On the front is the familiar home button (with its trademark box) and an edge-to-edge glass face, which houses the 4-inch retina display. Inside the phone is a steel frame, which doubles as an antenna, and adds to the rigid feel. </p>
<p>While the 5C shares most of the iPhone 5â€™s specs, Apple did upgrade the FaceTime camera. I had a chance to run my iPhone 5â€™s front facing-camera alongside the one on the iPhone 5C; the difference was startling, and is sure to change the video-calling experience.</p>
