        <div class="span6">
            <h3>What we are doing</h3>

            <p>We are building a next-generation version of the landmark
            <a href="/content/original-california-water-atlas">California Water
            Atlas</a></p>

            <ul>
                <li>Improving open data</li>

                <li>Building community &amp; sharing methods</li>

                <li>Creating high-end, legible visualizations</li>

                <li>Sharing open source code</li>

                <li>Adopting radically inclusive &amp; equitable ways of
                working</li>

                <li>Building a public work for Californians</li>

                <li>Incorporating science and encouraging accuracy</li>

                <li>Seeking peer-reviews</li>

                <li>Including historical data</li>

                <li>Healing damages of the past, non-violent
                communications</li>

                <li>Working for the future</li>
            </ul>
        </div>

        <div class="span4">
            <img alt="California Water Atlas book" src=
            "http://live-ca-statewater.gotpantheon.com/sites/default/files/styles/large/public/field/image/californiawateratlas_45-45.jpg">

            <h3>The original California Water Atlas</h3>

            <p>The California Water Atlas, a book published in 1979, continues
            to inspire map-makers and water lovers alike. We are making a new
            version of this Atlas for our generation as a public work for
            Californians using dynamic maps, open data and lots of citizen
            involvement. <a href=
            "/content/original-california-water-atlas">Learn more</a></p>
        </div>
    </div>

    <div class="row-fluid">
        <div class="span6">
            <h3>Why this project matters</h3>

            <blockquote>
                <p>"The New California Water Atlas could catalyze a shift in California water policy and governance that will make California water policy capable of supporting the state’s natural resources, human population and agricultural sector in perpetuity. That's why we're funding it."<br><br><small>Huey Johnson, President Resource Renewal
                Institute, former Secretary of Resource for the State of
                California (1978-1982), Advisory Group for the original
                California Water Atlas</small></p>
            </blockquote>

            <div class="divider-h"></div>

            <blockquote>
                <p>"The NCWA is filling an essential need by making it easier for Californians to understand who uses how much water where. That's not happening now, and we see the results in lawsuits, collapsing ecosystems, and overspending on attempts to compensate for mismanagement. Support the NCWA and support our future."<br><br><small>David Zetland, Project Advisor</small></p>
            </blockquote>

            <div class="divider-h"></div>

            <blockquote>
                <p>"How do we re-prioritize a water system designed by previous generations to meet the unique challenges of our time?  The New California Water Atlas is the much needed conduit between government and citizens to aid in facilitating dialogue, transparency, and data sharing about future social, environmental, and economic priorities for our water in California."<br><br><small>Laci Videmsky, Project Director</small></p>
            </blockquote>

            <span class="inset"><a href=

            <h3>What we are making</h3>

            <p>The original California Water Atlas described the water system
            of California through many maps and infographics.</p>

            <p>These are proposed interactive projects that are being created
            as a new Public Work for Californians.</p>

            <p>As an open source and collaborative effort, there are plenty of
            maps to make. Please help. We need all kinds of expertise:
            programmers, designers, scientists, writers, documentary-makers and
            more.</p>
