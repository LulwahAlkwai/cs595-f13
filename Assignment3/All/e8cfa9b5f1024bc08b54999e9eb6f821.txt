					<p><a href="http://9to5mac.files.wordpress.com/2013/09/screen-shot-2013-09-12-at-5-25-35-pm.png"><img title="Microsoft offering up $200 discounts on Surface with iPad trade-in" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-287351" alt="Screen Shot 2013-09-12 at 5.25.35 PM" src="http://s0.wp.com/wp-content/themes/vip/plugins/lazy-load/images/1x1.trans.gif?m=1322061781g" data-lazy-src="http://9to5mac.files.wordpress.com/2013/09/screen-shot-2013-09-12-at-5-25-35-pm.png?w=704&#038;h=459" width="704" height="459" /><noscript><img title="Microsoft offering up $200 discounts on Surface with iPad trade-in" class="aligncenter size-large wp-image-287351" alt="Screen Shot 2013-09-12 at 5.25.35 PM" src="http://9to5mac.files.wordpress.com/2013/09/screen-shot-2013-09-12-at-5-25-35-pm.png?w=704&#038;h=459" width="704" height="459" /></noscript></a></p>
<p>Microsoft has <a href="http://content.microsoftstore.com/en-us/offers?WT.mc_id=PromoEmail_iPadTradein_9-5-13_GetDetails#offer-tablet-trade">recently kicked off a new campaign</a> that <a href="http://news.cnet.com/8301-10805_3-57602719-75/microsoft-seeks-ipad-trade-ins/?part=rss&amp;subj=news&amp;tag=title">offers iPad users a $200 Microsoft Store gift card</a> to trade-in their Apple tablet. While the gift card is applicable to any item inside of Microsoft&#8217;s stores, the company is positioning the offer as push for sales of the Surface tablet. The deal ends on October 27, 2013.</p>
<p>This campaign is just one aspect of Microsoft&#8217;s attempts at trumping Apple&#8217;s iPad. Over the past several months, Microsoft has been running TV ads that position Windows 8 for tablets the Microsoft Surface against the iPad. These <a href="http://9to5mac.com/2013/05/23/microsoft-hits-ipad-again-this-time-about-hardware-specs-multitasking-printing/">ads have highlighted the multitasking capabilities of Windows 8</a>, and have <a href="http://9to5mac.com/2013/05/22/latest-windows-8-tablet-commercial-uses-siri-to-mock-ipad-ads-pricing-capabilities/">panned the iPad for the lack of a kickstand and USB ports. </a></p>
<p>As for the Surface, the tablet has essentially been a flop, leading the company to take a <a href="http://9to5mac.com/2013/07/19/even-after-disastrous-900m-write-down-on-discounted-surfaces-microsoft-still-attacking-ipad-in-new-ad-video/">$900 million write-off on the device</a>. Nonetheless, the <a href="http://allthingsd.com/20130909/microsoft-to-introduce-second-generation-of-surface-products-at-sept-23-new-york-event/">company will be upgrading</a> the more-than-year-old tablet later this month. Apple, for its part, is expected to unleash its new iPads about a month later.</p>
