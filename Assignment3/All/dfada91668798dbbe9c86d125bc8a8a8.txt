					<p>Command Center for iOS 7 is great: Simple access to often used settings and apps. It&#8217;s enabled on your Lock Screen by default. Super handy, right? Yes but that means anyone could disable your device radio even if you have a passcode lock.</p>
				</div>

	<section class="body entry-content" itemprop="articleBody">
		<div id="post-content-691678">
			<p>One of the newest iOS 7 features is certainly a time saver, but it can also make your lost iOS device harder to find.</p>
<p>Yesterday, I mentioned <a href="http://gigaom.com/2013/09/18/5-great-features-in-ios-7-youll-want-to-try-right-away/">the new Control Center as one of the five new iOS 7 features you&#8217;ll want to try right away</a>. I still believe it adds to the experience, however, by default, it&#8217;s not secure: Lose your iPhone or iPad and someone can keep the Find My iPhone app from locating your device.</p>
<p>Here&#8217;s how. By default, the new Control Center is made available on your lock screen without a passcode needed. Sure, that&#8217;s handy. Just slide up on your handset to open the camera app, enable AirDrop, or turn on Do Not Disturb. But one of the available options is to enable Airplane Mode.</p>
<p>I&#8217;d welcome that for a flight, and why not? No need to unlock the phone and fumble in Settings to turn off the phone&#8217;s radios. Except that anyone can turn off the phone&#8217;s radios in the default mode, even if you have a passcode lock setup in iOS 7.</p>
<p style="text-align:center;"><a href="http://gigaom2.files.wordpress.com/2013/09/find-my-iphone.jpg"><img  style="border:1px solid black;" alt="can't find my iphone" src="http://gigaom2.files.wordpress.com/2013/09/find-my-iphone.jpg?w=442&#038;h=473" width="442" height="473" class="aligncenter  wp-image-691692" /></a></p>
<p>If that happens, Find My iPhone essentially becomes useless. I tested the scenario with every Find My iPhone option but they were no help: Find My Phone, Play Sound, Lost Mode and Erase iPhone. (Note that I didn&#8217;t actually enable the Erase iPhone mode because Apple warned the phone would be erased when it re-connects to the internet.)</p>
<p>For now, I&#8217;ve disabled Control Center on the lock screen. You can easily do this too: Go to Settings, Control Center and disable the &#8220;Access on Lock Screen&#8221; option. Again, on my iPhone 5 and my wife&#8217;s iPhone 4S, this was enabled by default. We both have passcode locks on our phones and in both cases, we were able to enable Airplane mode without any security check.</p>
<p><img  style="border:1px solid black;" alt="Command Center iOS 7 settings" src="http://gigaom2.files.wordpress.com/2013/09/command.jpg?w=448&#038;h=529" width="448" height="529" class="aligncenter  wp-image-691688" /></p>
<p>Is this a huge security hole? No, I&#8217;d call it more of a security awareness issue.</p>
<p>Why? Because a thief could essentially accomplish the same thing by simply pulling the SIM card out of your device or by turning it off. But I&#8217;d like to see Apple consider requiring the passcode for Airplane mode through Control Center. I don&#8217;t want anyone cutting off my iPhone from the web but me, especially if I lose it.</p>
<p><em>This post was updated at 2:04pm PT to properly reflect the name of Control Center.</em></p>
