			<span class="lia-link-navigation blog-article-link lia-link-disabled" id="link_25">Virgin Atlantic Launches World's First-Ever Glass-Bott<wbr />omed Plane</span>
		</h2>

						<p>
														 
						<div class="lia-message-body-content"><P><IMG src="/t5/image/serverpage/image-id/2364i5B788FB970A57F9A/image-size/original?v=mpbl-1&amp;px=-1" border="0" alt="Virgin Atlantic's glass-bottomed plane.jpg" title="Virgin Atlantic's glass-bottomed plane.jpg" align="middle" /></P>
<P>&nbsp;</P>
<P>As you may have seen in the <A href="http://www.mirror.co.uk/news/uk-news/virgin-launch-new-jet-see-through-1795500" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">national press</A>, after nearly 30 years of providing the ultimate long-haul flying experience, Virgin Atlantic and our founder Richard Branson are once again flying in the face of ordinary by announcing the creation of a world first in aircraft innovation. This technological innovation coincides with the start of our first ever domestic service, Little Red.</P>
<P>&nbsp;</P>
<P>Today we've announced that our world-class engineering crew have been secretly working on the design and production of the world's first-ever glass-bottomed plane, created to ensure passengers can enjoy both an unparalleled flying experience, as well as a selection of stunning landscapes from the comfort of their seats.</P>
<P>&nbsp;</P>
<P>The new aircraft will offer every passenger the chance of a bird's eye view with an extra special opportunity to look down on the beautiful scenery of Great Britain as they fly.</P>
<P>&nbsp;</P>
<P>The project has been personally funded by Richard Branson and overseen by Virgin Atlantic's own Innovation team, who have been hard at work on this top secret project for several years. Richard set himself a personal goal to change the face of flying forever when he started Virgin Atlantic Airways in 1984 and this is one of many in the long line of aviation breakthroughs.</P>
<P>&nbsp;</P>
<P><IMG src="/t5/image/serverpage/image-id/2366iF7B2A523893E9103/image-size/original?v=mpbl-1&amp;px=-1" border="0" alt="Diagram of Virgin Atlantic's glass-bottomed plane .jpg" title="Diagram of Virgin Atlantic's glass-bottomed plane .jpg" align="middle" /></P>
<P>&nbsp;</P>
<P>The service will be open for bookings shortly and if it proves popular there are plans to explore options for adding a glass-bottomed plane on routes to long-haul travel destinations too.</P>
<P>&nbsp;</P>
<P>The aircraft will look identical to the standard Airbus A320 that is currently being used on Little Red's domestic routes, but will have a clear strip made from aviation-standard glass in place of the traditional aisle. This ensures the aisle in the plane's underbelly is completely see through, allowing passengers to look down directly as the plane makes its journey through the sky.</P>
<P>&nbsp;</P>
<P>Cabin crew will also be specially trained to assist with any fliers who may be nervous travelling on the innovative aircraft. As an alternative, those anxious fliers are urged to book onto the two other daily flights from Heathrow to Aberdeen running as part of Little Red's scheduled timetable.</P>
<P>&nbsp;</P>
<P>"I'm thrilled to announce that Virgin has created another world-first with the introduction of the technology required to produce the world's first glass-bottomed plane," said Richard Branson, Virgin Atlantic founder and president. "In 1984 we started the wonderful airline that is Virgin Atlantic Airways. I am incredibly proud of yet another aviation breakthrough which has been years in the making. I can't wait to experience the first flight for myself with my family and other natural born explorers."</P>
<P>&nbsp;</P>
<P>"I have asked the rest of the Virgin companies to get on board and support my latest quest to help us support the new domestic Scottish flight path. This really is a team effort from all corners of Virgin.</P>
<P>&nbsp;</P>
<P><IMG src="/t5/image/serverpage/image-id/2368i3A4A893B46EE0900/image-size/original?v=mpbl-1&amp;px=-1" border="0" alt="Virgin Atlantic builds first ever glass-bottomed plane.jpg" title="Virgin Atlantic builds first ever glass-bottomed plane.jpg" align="middle" /></P>
<P>&nbsp;</P>
<P>"2012 was a year of celebrating what is brilliant about Great Britain and I'm excited that in 2013 we are continuing this uplifting spirit by developing an experience that will enable Little Red passengers to appreciate the beauty of the British landscape. And with an unrivalled view of Scotland I hope this gives Scottish tourism an even bigger boost.</P>
<P>&nbsp;</P>
<P>To see the entire Virgin group activity to support the glass-bottomed plane and further information on this exciting announcement, please visit <A href="http://www.virgin.com/richard-branson/blog." target="_blank" rel="nofollow">www.virgin.com/richard-branson/blog.</A></P>
<P>&nbsp;</P>
<P><A href="http://www.virgin-atlantic.com/gb/en/the-virgin-experience/little-red.html" target="_self">Virgin Atlantic Little Red</A> is our new UK domestic service, offering great value, full service multiple daily flights between London and Manchester, London and Aberdeen and London and Edinburgh. Little Red flights can be <A href="http://www.virgin-atlantic.com/gb/en/book-your-travel/book-your-flight.html" target="_self">booked now</A>, bringing you closer to all our destinations.</P></div>
