<section class='article-content'>

<p>Just like clockwork, <a href="http://mashable.com/category/apple/">Apple</a>'s online store went down Tuesday morning ahead of the company's big event.</p>
<p>Apple is holding a media event at 10 a.m. PT/1 p.m. ET at its campus in Cupertino, Calif., where it is expected to unveil two new <a href="http://mashable.com/category/iphone/">iPhone</a> models and the latest version of iOS (you can <a href="http://mashable.com/2013/09/09/apple-iphone-event-live-blog/" data-crackerjax="#post-slider">follow our live blog here</a>). The event marks Apple's first major product launch in nearly a year.</p>

<section class="gallery" data-display-mode="gallery" data-id="759" data-slide-title-pos="" data-slug="verizon-iphone-apps"><header><h1>10 Must-Download Apps for New iPhone Owners</h1>
</header><ol class="slides">
<li class="slide" data-content-source="Gallery - Default" data-id="522f2e3b97b2f827080056cd" data-skip-rerender="false" data-thumb="http://rack.0.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEyLzA5LzI0LzAxXzI0XzM2Xzc1X2ZpbGUKcAl0aHVtYgkxODB4MTQwIwplCWpwZw/30e7d746.jpg">
<figure><img alt="F0177b01" src="http://rack.0.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEyLzA5LzI0LzAxXzI0XzM2Xzc1X2ZpbGUKcAl0aHVtYgk4NTB4NTkwPgplCWpwZw/f0177b01.jpg"></figure><div class="meta">
<h2 class="title">1. <a href="http://itunes.apple.com/us/app/instagram/id389801252?mt=8&amp;uo=4" target="_blank">Instagram</a>
</h2>
<div class="caption">
<p>This photo sharing app boasts compatibility with Twitter, Tumblr, Facebook and Foursquare, and has lots of fun filters. Instagram isn't just a great way to share your own photos, it's a great way to see what photos your friends are taking. As a freebie, this app is a no-brainer. It's the kind of quality offering that has made the App Store such as huge success.</p>
<p><strong>Cost:</strong> Free</p>
</div>
<div class="credit">

</div>
</div>
</li>
<li class="slide" data-content-source="Gallery - Default" data-id="522f2e3b97b2f827080056ce" data-skip-rerender="false" data-thumb="http://rack.0.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEyLzA5LzI0LzAxXzI0XzM2XzcwOF9maWxlCnAJdGh1bWIJMTgweDE0MCMKZQlqcGc/6246cc75.jpg">
<figure><img alt="3f08c89a" src="http://rack.0.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEyLzA5LzI0LzAxXzI0XzM2XzcwOF9maWxlCnAJdGh1bWIJODUweDU5MD4KZQlqcGc/3f08c89a.jpg"></figure><div class="meta">
<h2 class="title">2. <a href="http://itunes.apple.com/us/app/cut-the-rope/id380293530?mt=8&amp;uo=4" target="_blank">Cut the Rope</a>
</h2>
<div class="caption">
<p>Starring Om Nom, a little green monster that loves candy, <i>Cut the Rope</i> is a surprisingly addictive physics game in which you are required to cut said rope in order to drop a sweet into Om Nom's mouth. Despite the cutesy premise and child-like graphics, the game has received some serious accolades and, as an App Store exclusive (for now), is a bit of a jewel in the iPhone's casual gaming crown.</p>
<p><strong>Cost:</strong> $0.99</p>
</div>
<div class="credit">

</div>
</div>
</li>
<li class="slide" data-content-source="Gallery - Default" data-id="522f2e3b97b2f827080056cf" data-skip-rerender="false" data-thumb="http://rack.3.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEyLzA5LzI0LzAxXzI0XzM3XzY3X2ZpbGUKcAl0aHVtYgkxODB4MTQwIwplCWpwZw/03544e51.jpg">
<figure><img alt="1635309a" src="http://rack.3.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEyLzA5LzI0LzAxXzI0XzM3XzY3X2ZpbGUKcAl0aHVtYgk4NTB4NTkwPgplCWpwZw/1635309a.jpg"></figure><div class="meta">
<h2 class="title">3. <a href="http://itunes.apple.com/us/app/goodreader-for-iphone/id306277111?mt=8&amp;uo=4" target="_blank">GoodReader</a>
</h2>
<div class="caption">
<p>The iPhone 4 has built-in PDF support, but GoodReader works better for large files and has many more features, including support for some MS Office formats. It's a great all-purpose document viewer, file manager, PDF reader and annotator, so if you're planning to do more with your iPhone than play games and take pics, this will start you off on the right track.</p>
<p><strong>Cost:</strong> $2.99</p>
</div>
<div class="credit">

</div>
</div>
</li>
<li class="slide" data-content-source="Gallery - Default" data-id="522f2e3b97b2f827080056d0" data-skip-rerender="false" data-thumb="http://rack.1.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEyLzA5LzI0LzAxXzI0XzM2XzE2MV9maWxlCnAJdGh1bWIJMTgweDE0MCMKZQlqcGc/f9d3d191.jpg">
<figure><img alt="6dfbefc4" src="http://rack.1.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEyLzA5LzI0LzAxXzI0XzM2XzE2MV9maWxlCnAJdGh1bWIJODUweDU5MD4KZQlqcGc/6dfbefc4.jpg"></figure><div class="meta">
<h2 class="title">4. <a href="http://itunes.apple.com/us/app/hipstamatic/id342115564?mt=8&amp;uo=4" target="_blank">Hipstamatic</a>
</h2>
<div class="caption">
<p>Apple's iPhone app of the year for 2010 turns the mobile into a toy camera. More than just a filter pack, the photographs taken with the app have a genuine toy camera feel, with options to adjust the film, lenses and even type of flash effect. In fact, you can even <a href="http://mashable.com/2010/09/17/hipstamatic-real-prints/">order analog prints</a> of your snapshots from within the app.</p>
<p><strong>Cost:</strong> $1.99</p>
</div>
<div class="credit">

</div>
</div>
</li>
<li class="slide" data-content-source="Gallery - Default" data-id="522f2e3b97b2f827080056d1" data-skip-rerender="false" data-thumb="http://rack.0.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEyLzA5LzI0LzAxXzI0XzM2XzU3MV9maWxlCnAJdGh1bWIJMTgweDE0MCMKZQlqcGc/d0b8b8d7.jpg">
<figure><img alt="B655253c" src="http://rack.1.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEyLzA5LzI0LzAxXzI0XzM2XzU3MV9maWxlCnAJdGh1bWIJODUweDU5MD4KZQlqcGc/b655253c.jpg"></figure><div class="meta">
<h2 class="title">5. <a href="http://itunes.apple.com/us/app/reeder/id325502379?mt=8&amp;uo=4" target="_blank">Reeder</a>
</h2>
<div class="caption">
<p>Are you an RSS feed fan? Then swallow the cost and download Reeder, one of the very best RSS apps around. The app makes Google Reader finally worth using on mobile, and browsing is easy thanks to the intuitive controls. Besides catching up on your daily dose of news, you can share items to Twitter and Facebook.</p>
<p><strong>Cost:</strong> $2.99</p>
</div>
<div class="credit">

</div>
</div>
</li>
<li class="slide" data-content-source="Gallery - Default" data-id="522f2e3b97b2f827080056d2" data-skip-rerender="false" data-thumb="http://rack.3.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEyLzA5LzI0LzAxXzI0XzM2Xzc3MF9maWxlCnAJdGh1bWIJMTgweDE0MCMKZQlqcGc/8b04fb0c.jpg">
<figure><img alt="A916d00f" src="http://rack.1.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEyLzA5LzI0LzAxXzI0XzM2Xzc3MF9maWxlCnAJdGh1bWIJODUweDU5MD4KZQlqcGc/a916d00f.jpg"></figure><div class="meta">
<h2 class="title">6. <a href="http://itunes.apple.com/us/app/hipporemote-pro-wireless-keyboard/id320157370?mt=8&amp;uo=4" target="_blank">HippoRemote</a>
</h2>
<div class="caption">
<p>An app that really demonstrates what the iPhone is capable of, HippoRemote acts as a remote control for your PC, Mac or Apple TV. It's pretty smart as a companion device, especially if you have a laptop or desktop hooked up to your TV. The touchpad on it controls the mouse, there are programmable macros for certain actions, and it's a snap to set up. If you can't afford the price tag, or don't need all that functionality, then Apple's <a href="http://itunes.apple.com/us/app/remote/id284417350?mt=8&amp;uo=4" target="_blank">free Remote app</a> also works quite well for controlling an Apple TV or iTunes.</p>
<p><strong>Cost:</strong> $4.99</p>
</div>
<div class="credit">

</div>
</div>
</li>
<li class="slide" data-content-source="Gallery - Default" data-id="522f2e3b97b2f827080056d3" data-skip-rerender="false" data-thumb="http://rack.0.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEyLzA5LzI0LzAxXzI0XzM3XzQyMF9maWxlCnAJdGh1bWIJMTgweDE0MCMKZQlqcGc/32627e9e.jpg">
<figure><img alt="8f9ccb1e" src="http://rack.0.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEyLzA5LzI0LzAxXzI0XzM3XzQyMF9maWxlCnAJdGh1bWIJODUweDU5MD4KZQlqcGc/8f9ccb1e.jpg"></figure><div class="meta">
<h2 class="title">7. <a href="http://itunes.apple.com/us/app/netflix/id363590051?mt=8&amp;uo=4" target="_blank">Netflix</a>
</h2>
<div class="caption">
<p>If you're a Netflix "Unlimited" member, then this app is a must with a capital M. It'll allow you to manage your Instant Queue from your iPhone. Better still, you can stream TV shows and movies whenever you want, and the really clever part is that you can resume watching where you left off on your TV or PC. For non-Netflixers, the <a href="http://itunes.apple.com/us/app/hulu-plus/id376510438?mt=8&amp;uo=4" target="_blank">Hulu Plus iPhone app</a> is also worth a look for paid-up subscribers of that service.</p>
<p><strong>Cost:</strong> Free</p>
</div>
<div class="credit">

</div>
</div>
</li>
<li class="slide" data-content-source="Gallery - Default" data-id="522f2e3b97b2f827080056d4" data-skip-rerender="false" data-thumb="http://rack.0.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEyLzA5LzI0LzAxXzI0XzM2XzI4NV9maWxlCnAJdGh1bWIJMTgweDE0MCMKZQlqcGc/6d14732a.jpg">
<figure><img alt="B8585d11" src="http://rack.2.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEyLzA5LzI0LzAxXzI0XzM2XzI4NV9maWxlCnAJdGh1bWIJODUweDU5MD4KZQlqcGc/b8585d11.jpg"></figure><div class="meta">
<h2 class="title">8. <a href="http://itunes.apple.com/us/app/8mm-vintage-camera/id406541444?mt=8&amp;uo=4" target="_blank">8mm Vintage Camera</a>
</h2>
<div class="caption">
<p>Think of 8mm as Hipstamatic for video. It has "live view" functionality so you can see what you're shooting, and there's even "projector" sounds to achieve authentically aged footage. You can apply cool filters and effects to the video you shoot and then save it or upload it straight to YouTube. The app is easy to use and the results are surprisingly good.</p>
<p><strong>Cost:</strong> $1.99</p>
</div>
<div class="credit">

</div>
</div>
</li>
<li class="slide" data-content-source="Gallery - Default" data-id="522f2e3b97b2f827080056d5" data-skip-rerender="false" data-thumb="http://rack.0.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEyLzA5LzI0LzAxXzI0XzM2XzY1NV9maWxlCnAJdGh1bWIJMTgweDE0MCMKZQlqcGc/04c07f54.jpg">
<figure><img alt="Bf6f968f" src="http://rack.2.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEyLzA5LzI0LzAxXzI0XzM2XzY1NV9maWxlCnAJdGh1bWIJODUweDU5MD4KZQlqcGc/bf6f968f.jpg"></figure><div class="meta">
<h2 class="title">9. <a href="http://itunes.apple.com/us/app/fruit-ninja/id362949845?mt=8&amp;uo=4" target="_blank">Fruit Ninja</a>
</h2>
<div class="caption">
<p>Not quite as cute as <i>Cut the Rope</i>, but no less addictive, <i>Fruit Ninja</i> brings some wholesome, tasty fruit slicing fun, and uses the touchscreen to great advantage. We've seen 4-year-olds and 40-year-olds get sucked into this game -- it's fun, messy and boasts great satisfying sound effects. Three million downloads later, App Store customers seem to agree.</p>
<p><strong>Cost:</strong> $0.99</p>
</div>
<div class="credit">

</div>
</div>
</li>
<li class="slide" data-content-source="Gallery - Default" data-id="522f2e3b97b2f827080056d6" data-skip-rerender="false" data-thumb="http://rack.2.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEyLzA5LzI0LzAxXzI0XzM3XzM2MF9maWxlCnAJdGh1bWIJMTgweDE0MCMKZQlqcGc/3267d91d.jpg">
<figure><img alt="D2f292b1" src="http://rack.3.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEyLzA5LzI0LzAxXzI0XzM3XzM2MF9maWxlCnAJdGh1bWIJODUweDU5MD4KZQlqcGc/d2f292b1.jpg"></figure><div class="meta">
<h2 class="title">10. <a href="http://itunes.apple.com/us/app/google-mobile-app/id284815942?mt=8&amp;uo=4" target="_blank">Google Mobile App</a>
</h2>
<div class="caption">
<p>If you're coming to the Verizon iPhone from a background in Android, this app may will help with the transition. The free Google Mobile App will alleviate any homesickness with Google Goggles, search by voice, the My Location map shortcut and various Google-powered search options. Quick links to other useful Google applications also make this a worthwhile download for non-Android folk.</p>
<p><strong>Cost:</strong> Free</p>
</div>
<div class="credit">

</div>
