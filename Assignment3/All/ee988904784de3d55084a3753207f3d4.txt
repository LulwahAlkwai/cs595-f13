		<div><p>The Internet and all those who care about Aaron took a big step forward today: Rep. Zoe Lofgren and Sen. Ron Wyden just introduced "Aaron's Law", which would fix some of the worst parts of the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act,&nbsp;<strong>including those which make it a potential crime to violate terms of service agreements&nbsp;</strong>-- that fine print that nobody reads at the bottom of a website.</p>
<p>Add your name at right to list yourself as a "citizen cosponsor" of Aaron's Law, and we'll also let your lawmakers know that you want them to cosponsor the bill.</p>
<p>The CFAA is the law under which Aaron Swartz and other innovators and activists have been threatened with decades in prison. It is so broad that&nbsp;<strong>law enforcement says it criminalizes all sorts of mundane Internet use:</strong>&nbsp;Potentially even breaking a website's fine print terms of service agreement. Don't set up a Myspace page for your cat. Don't fudge your height on a dating site. Don't share your Facebook password with anybody: You could be committing a federal crime. (Read more&nbsp;<a href="https://www.eff.org/issues/cfaa" target="_blank">here</a>.)</p>
<p>If you're already on&nbsp;<strong>Facebook</strong>,&nbsp;<a href="http://facebook.com/share.php?u=http://act.demandprogress.org/act/aarons_law_intro/%3Fsource=fb" target="_blank">click here to share with your friends.</a></p>
<p>If you're already on&nbsp;<strong>Twitter</strong>,&nbsp;<a href="http://twitter.com/share?text=Pls%20RT%3A%20%23cfaa%20%23aaronswartz%20Aaron%27s%20Law%20just%20intro%27ed.%20Become%20citizen%20cosponsor%3A&amp;url=http://act.demandprogress.org/act/aarons_law_intro/%3Freferring_akid%3Da8908746.194196.j3jmzW%26source%3Dauto-tw&amp;via=demandprogress" target="_blank">click here to tweet about the campaign</a>.</p>
<h3>Just add your name at right to be counted as a supporter of Aaron's Law.</h3>
