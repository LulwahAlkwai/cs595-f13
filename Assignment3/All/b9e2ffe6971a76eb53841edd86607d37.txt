Want to use science to add punch to your pieces? Want to know how to figure out if a study is worth covering, or how to make sense of it quickly? You don't need a Ph.D. to write well about science. By learning some basics about how science works in the real world and what makes scientific information newsworthy, you can add substance and credibility to your work.</span></p>  <p class="MsoNormal"><span>This online class covers:</span></p>  <ul><li class="MsoNormal"><span>the best places      to find science news</span></li><li class="MsoNormal"><span>how to find the      right expert, not just the nearest expert</span></li><li class="MsoNormal"><span>how to quickly      vet a study</span></li><li class="MsoNormal"><span>the art of      explaining science</span></li><li class="MsoNormal"><span>narrative      science writing</span></li><li class="MsoNormal"><span>pitching science      stories</span></li></ul>  <p class="MsoNormal"><span>The class will feature readings, lessons and assignments, which will be posted and discussed in a password-protected forum. Several readings will be drawn from <em><span style="font-family: Cambria">A Field Guide for Science Writers</span></em>, a how-to book from the National Association of Science Writers that costs about $20.</span></p>  <p class="MsoNormal"><span>Dan Ferber is an award-winning journalist whose writing on science, technology, health and sustainability has appeared in <em><span style="font-family: Cambria">Reader's Digest</span></em>, <em><span style="font-family: Cambria">Popular Science</span></em>, <em><span style="font-family: Cambria">Wired</span></em>, <em><span style="font-family: Cambria">Scientific American, Audubon</span></em>, <em><span style="font-family: Cambria">Nature Conservancy, Women's Health</span></em> and many other publications. He's a correspondent for <em><span style="font-family: Cambria">Science</span></em> magazine, the coauthor of the award-winning book, <em><span style="font-family: Cambria">Changing Planet, Changing Health</span></em>, and an adjunct instructor of journalism at Indiana University.</span></p>  <p class="MsoNormal"><span>$249 for Freelance Success members, $299 for nonmembers.
