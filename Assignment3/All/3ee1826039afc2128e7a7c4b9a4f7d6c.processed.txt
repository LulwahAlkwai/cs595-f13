What do you call a group of ...?

   Many of these terms belong to 15th-century lists of 'proper terms',
   notably that in the Book of St Albans attributed to Dame Juliana Barnes
   (1486). Many of these are fanciful or humorous terms which probably
   never had any real currency, but have been taken up by antiquarian
   writers, notably Joseph Strutt in Sports and Pastimes of England
   (1801).

People

   a blush of boys
   a drunkship of cobblers
   a hastiness of cooks
   a stalk of foresters
   an observance of hermits
   a bevy of ladies
   a faith of merchants
   a superfluity of nuns
   a malapertness (= impertinence) of pedlars
   a pity of prisoners
   a glozing (= fawning) of taverners
