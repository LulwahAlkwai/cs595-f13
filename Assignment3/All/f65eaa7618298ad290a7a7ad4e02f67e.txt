<section class='article-content'>

<p>One viral video and several twists and turns later, Matthew Cordle finally kept his promise to accept responsibility for his involvement in a fatal crash.</p>
<p>The 22-year-old pleaded guilty to charges of aggravated vehicular homicide and driving while intoxicated in an Ohio courtroom Wednesday morning, a week after he pleaded not guilty <a href="http://mashable.com/2013/09/11/youtube-confession-not-guilty/" data-crackerjax="#post-slider">due to a court procedure</a> at his arraignment.</p>
<div class="see-also"><p>See also: <a href="http://mashable.com/2013/09/06/youtube-confess-crash/" data-crackerjax="#post-slider">"I Killed a Man": A YouTube Confession for a Drunken Car Crash</a></p></div>
<p>"I drank so much I was blacked out," Cordle told the judge, who asked Cordle why he could not remember the details of the night of the accident on June 22.</p>
<p>Prosecutor Ron O'Brien told the court Cordle's blood alcohol level was measured at .19, more than twice the legal limit, on the night of the accident. He said hospital attendants reported <span class="microcontent" data-fragment="cordle-did-not-realize" data-description="Cordle did not realize he had been in a car accident and became hostile when initially asked for a blood sample." data-micro="1">Cordle did not realize he had been in a car accident and became hostile when initially asked for a blood sample.</span></p>
<p>"He's waiving all of his constitutional rights, and he's doing so in an expedited manner, in order to honor the promises he's made," W. Martin Midian, one of Cordle's attorneys, told the judge.</p>
<p>Cordle's <a href="http://mashable.com/category/youtube/">YouTube</a> confession has amassed more than 2 million views since it was posted Sept. 3. In the video, he admits to driving drunk at the time of the crash, when Cordle drove his pickup truck the wrong way on I-670 in Ohio and crashed into Vincent Canzani's Jeep. Canzani, 61, was pronounced dead at the scene, the <em>Columbus Dispatch</em> <a href="http://www.dispatch.com/content/stories/local/2013/06/23/1-killed-1-injured-in-i-670-wrong-way-crash.html" target="_blank">reported</a>.</p>
<p>Many were quick to question Cordle's sincerity when he entered a not guilty plea at his arraignment on Sept. 11, but Franklin County assistant prosecutor Keith McGrath said Cordle was simply following standard operating procedure. </p>
<p>"This is not unusual," McGrath told <em>Mashable</em>. "Every person that came through that arraignment court entered a not guilty plea. It's how you get assigned to a random judge here in Franklin County. You have to enter that not guilty plea first."</p>
<p>"When I get charged, I will plead guilty and take full responsibility for everything I've done to Vincent and his family," Cordle says in the video titled "I Killed a Man," which was posted on the website <em>Because I Said I Would</em>. The site encourages people to make promise cards to "remember the importance of your commitments and goals."</p>
<p>Cordle contacted Alex Sheen, the founder of <em>Because I Said I Would</em>, on <a href="http://mashable.com/category/facebook/">Facebook</a>. Sheen produced and published the confessional video. </p>
<p>"I thought, like anybody, that he might back down — he might try to pull something that would get him a lighter sentence or something," Sheen told <em>Mashable</em> during a phone conversation last week. "Matt has been sticking true to his word, taking what's coming to him — what he deserves."</p>
<p>At the hearing Wednesday, <span class="microcontent" data-fragment="cordles-attorneys-said-he" data-description="Cordle's attorneys said he had made a &quot;commitment to sobriety,&quot;" data-micro="1">Cordle's attorneys said he had made a "commitment to sobriety,"</span> starting with voluntarily entering himself into a two-week rehabilitation program when he was released from the hospital.</p>
<p>Cordle faces anywhere from two to eight-and-a-half years in prison and a fine of up to $1,675. In addition, the aggravated vehicular homicide charge brings a mandatory lifetime drivers license suspension. His sentencing hearing is scheduled for Oct. 10.</p>
