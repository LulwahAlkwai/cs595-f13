<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
Who Cares If It's Been Tried Before?
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-8170007885096019438' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;" trbidi="on">
When Dropbox launched, there were at least half a dozen other "<a href="http://techcrunch.com/2006/01/31/the-online-storage-gang/">online storage</a>" businesses in existence. &nbsp;The Apple iPod entered a market litered with <a href="http://gizmodo.com/5846384/the-zune-player-is-dead-for-real-this-time">crappy</a> MP3 players. &nbsp;Google was famously the <a href="http://searchenginewatch.com/article/2064954/Where-Are-They-Now-Search-Engines-Weve-Known-Loved">Nth Internet search engine</a>. &nbsp;Stripe launched into a market crowded with Braintree and Paypal, and before that Authorize.net and others. &nbsp;Uber was not the first way you could order a taxi, although their spin on black cars was unique. &nbsp;Facebook was yet another social network in a market "saturated" by <a href="http://techcrunch.com/2012/11/19/myspace-music-streaming-service-no/">Myspace</a>, <a href="http://techcrunch.com/2011/06/29/from-social-network-pioneer-to-yet-another-gaming-site-friendster-reboots/">Friendster</a>, Orkut, <a href="http://techcrunch.com/2010/06/17/confirmed-criterion-capital-partners-acquires-bebo-from-aol/">Bebo</a>, <a href="http://techcrunch.com/2011/12/14/facebooks-leftovers-social-network-tagged-acquires-hi5/">Hi5</a> and others.<br />
<br />
Some good ideas have a lot of bad implementations before someone comes in and does it well enough to win big.<br />
<br />
Every large market, and every good idea, will have half a dozen others who are digging around the edges of it. &nbsp;Many of these companies will be doing something similar to your own idea, or will provide a service that at a high level sounds similar. &nbsp;In some cases these companies will win if they have a superior product or an unfair advantage in distribution (think Microsoft bundling browsers with their OS). &nbsp;In a lot of other cases, the market is still wide open even if at a high level it seems crowded or busy.<br />
<br />
A market that is&nbsp;perceived as crowded can actually be quite empty. &nbsp;No one is really providing a great product or service. &nbsp;These crappy competitors are getting some customer traction because the market needs a product similar to what they have. &nbsp;But they have not built anything good enough to defend against the next young Google or Facebook.<br />
<br />
Remember, just because someone has tried it before doesn't mean you can't make it work. &nbsp;Just because someone else is doing it, doesn't mean you won't win. &nbsp;You need to ask yourself is the competitor led by great people who can fast follow? &nbsp;Is their product and brand really strong? &nbsp;Do they have unfair or proprietary distribution? &nbsp;If the answer to these questions is no, then you may want to ignore the naysayers and go for it.<br />
