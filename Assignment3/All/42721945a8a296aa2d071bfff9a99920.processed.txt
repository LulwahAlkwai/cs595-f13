Motorola mocks iPhone 5s fingerprint scanner, forgets its own phone had a
fingerprint scanner first

   By [45]Zach Epstein on Sep 12, 2013 at 11:25 AM
   iPhone 5s Fingerprint Scanner
   [46]mobile
     * [47]Share on Twitter
     * [48]Share on Facebook
     * [49]Share on Google Plus
     * [50]Share on LinkedIn

   [51]mobile September 12, 2013 at 11:25 AM
   11:25 AM

   During [52]Apple's iPhone event this past Tuesday, a number of rival
   vendors took to Twitter in an attempt to have some fun at Apple's
   expense. Unfortunately, most of them live in glass houses. Among the
   merry smartphone makers was Nokia, which [53]poked fun as Apple's
   colorful new iPhone 5c lineup as it gets ready to [54]offload the
   failed handset business it couldn't figure out how to revive, and
   [55]Motorola, which made fun of the embedded fingerprint scanner on the
   [56]iPhone 5s. The problem with Motorola mocking Apple's new
   fingerprint scanner, of course, is the fact that Motorola itself
   launched a smartphone with an embedded fingerprint reader back in 2011.

   "Remember that one time you were stoked to give your fingerprints? Us
   either," Motorola wrote in a tweet. The vendor, which managed an
   [57]operating loss of more than $300 million last quarter, then linked
   to its new promotional video, which promises that [58]the Moto X is the
   answer to the "lazy phone."

     Remember that one time you were stoked to give your fingerprints? Us
     either. [59]#LazyPhone [60]http://t.co/ckuu7otnJM

     -- Motorola Mobility (@Motorola) [61]September 10, 2013

   As [62]Forbes points out, however, Motorola itself launched a
   smartphone with an embedded fingerprint scanner -- and it wasn't even
   that long ago.

   The [63]Motorola Atrix 4G featured an in-built print reader that was
   embedded in the phone's unlock button. And [64]as we discussed in out
   Atrix 4G review back in February 2011, the scanner served the exact
   same purpose as Apple's fingerprint reader, allowing users to unlock
   the phone without having to enter a PIN code.

   Oops.
