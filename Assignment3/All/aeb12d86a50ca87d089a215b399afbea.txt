	<p>The pen is mightier than the sword, and the fate of nations has been altered by a well-written manifesto. Political movements have been launched by a single, passionate speech, and marketing departments spend millions of dollars coming up with the perfect three words to describe their products.</p>
<p>The fact is, language matters, and as CIO it is one of your most powerful tools in bringing IT together with the business. (And it&rsquo;s cheap.)</p>
<p>But before you change the language that your IT organization uses, you need to know what&rsquo;s working and what isn&rsquo;t. &nbsp;&nbsp;If every time you say the word &ldquo;infrastructure&rdquo; or &ldquo;cloud&rdquo; or &ldquo;agile&rdquo; or &ldquo;architecture&rdquo; you hear a collective groan around the steering committee table, you should probably find a different word.</p>
<p>My question to Heller Report readers this week: <em>What is the one word or phrase the business would like NEVER to hear from IT? </em>Please tell us using the Comments section below.<em><br /></em></p>
