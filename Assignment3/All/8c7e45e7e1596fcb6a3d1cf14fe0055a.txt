        <h1 class="Museo-500"><span class="orange">Ethics Tools</span><br />
        <span>What constitutes scientific</span><br />
<span>misconduct and breach of</span><br /><span>publishing ethics?</span></h1>
        <h2 class="Museo-700">Scientific misconduct and breach of publishing<br />ethics can take different shapes and forms, and<br />can be committed knowingly or unknowingly. </h2>
        
      <div class="ethicsToolkit first">
        <h2 class="Museo-300 orange">Webcast about ethics in research and publication</h2>
        <p class="Museo-300 medium noMargin">Our experts David Rew, Margaret Rees, and Sandy Florence have presented a webcast to help early career researchers understand the ethical boundaries in scientific research and publishing. The webcast can be freely watched on demand, after registration.</p>
        <a href="http://mediazone.brighttalk.com/event/ReedElsevier/0ef41260a5-6663-intro" target="_blank"><img src="images/webcastLink.png" alt="Ethics Webcasr: Click here to view the webcast" width="573" height="169" usemap="#Map" class="webcastButton" border="0" />

      <div class="ethicsToolkit first">
        <h2 class="Museo-300 orange">Examples of scientific misconduct</h2>
            <ul class="bullet Museo-300 medium">
                <li><span><a href="pdf/ETHICS_RF01a.pdf" target="_blank" class="pdf">Research fraud</a> - which includes fabrication (making up research data); and falsification (manipulation of existing research data, tables, or images)</span><sup>1</sup></li>
                <li><span>Improper use of humans or animals in research - which includes absent  or inadequate informed consent of human subjects, or maltreatment of laboratory animals</span><sup>2</sup></li>
            </ul>
        </div>
        
<div class="ethicsToolkit first">
          <h2 class="Museo-300 orange">Examples of breach of publications ethics</h2>
            <ul class="bullet Museo-300 medium">
                <li><span><a href="pdf/ETHICS_PLA01a.pdf" target="_blank" class="pdf">Plagiarism</a> - passing off another's work or idea as your own</span></li>
                <li><span><a href="pdf/ETHICS_SSUB01a.pdf" target="_blank" class="pdf">Duplicate submission</a> - submitting a paper simultaneously to more than one publication at a time</span></li>
                <li><span><a href="pdf/ETHICS_COI01a.pdf" target="_blank" class="pdf">Conflict of interest</a> - nondisclosure to the publication that you have a direct or indirect conflict that prevents you from being unbiased in your paper</span><sup>2</sup></li>
                <li><span><a href="pdf/ETHICS_AUTH01a.pdf" target="_blank" class="pdf">Authorship disputes</a> - deliberately misrepresenting a scientist's relationship to their work on a published paper</span></li>
                <li><span><a href="pdf/ETHICS_SS01a.pdf" target="_blank" class="pdf">Salami slicing</a> - the "slicing" of research that would form one meaningful paper into several different papers</span></li>
            </ul>
