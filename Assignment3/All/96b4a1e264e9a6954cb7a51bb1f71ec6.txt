						<h1>The chart Tim Cook doesn&#8217;t want you to see</h1>
						<p class="byline">
							By <span class="author inline-block"><a rel="author" href="http://qz.com/author/davidyanofskyquartz" title="Archive for David Yanofsky">David Yanofsky</a></span> &mdash; <span class="timestamp" title="September 10, 2013">September 10, 2013</span>
						</p>
					</header>
				</div>
				<div class="item-body">
					<div>
						<p>At Apple&#8217;s iPhone presentation today, Tim Cook <a href="http://live.theverge.com/apple-iphone-5s-liveblog/">presented</a> the following chart:</p>
<p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-122922" alt="iphone-sales" src="http://qzprod.files.wordpress.com/2013/09/iphone-sales.jpg?w=1020&#038;h=680" width="1020" height="680" /></p>
<p>At best the chart is misleading; at worst it&#8217;s disingenuous. The chart has no scale. It could be showing billions of iPhone sales or it could be showing hundreds of iPhone sales.</p>
<p>Furthermore, showing sales cumulatively tacitly overstates the number of iPhone users, since some iPhone purchases are replacing older or broken iPhones.  Many of the phones Apple is claiming as praiseworthy in this chart found the junkyard years ago.</p>
<p>Using data from Apple&#8217;s own quarterly reports filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission, I made a better chart:</p>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-122973" alt="LB_7986_adjusted_bw" src="http://qzprod.files.wordpress.com/2013/09/lb_7986_adjusted_bw.png?w=1020&#038;h=620" width="1020" height="620" /></p>
<p>The overlay shows that, although Apple can boast more than 400 million iPhones sold for all time, its quarterly sales of the product have declined over the last three quarters.</p>
<p>Also worth noting is that the chart shown gives a preview of Apple&#8217;s sales ahead of its fourth-quarter earnings release on October 21. Judging by the change in slope on its chart, quarterly sales of iPhones continued to decline.</p>
