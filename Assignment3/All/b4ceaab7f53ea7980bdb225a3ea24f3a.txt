					</ul><p>Koch Industries will not be buying the Tribune Company&#8217;s eight newspapers, which include the Chicago Tribune and the LA Times, The Daily Caller has learned.</p>
<p>Sources with knowledge of the business proceedings told The Daily Caller that Koch Industries, after conducting its due diligence, has not been interested in buying the newspapers for &#8220;a couple months.&#8221;</p>
<p>The company determined that purchasing the newspapers was &#8220;not economically viable&#8221; and that both parties walked away from the negotiations, they said.</p>
<p>Two of the Tribune Company&#8217;s digital assets in particular, CareerBuilder.com and Classified Ventures LLC., a source with knowledge of the proceedings told The Daily Caller, were discovered to be half of the company&#8217;s revenue.</p>
<p>&#8220;They&#8217;re basically such an important part of the revenue,&#8221; the source confirmed, &#8220;in a way that if they sold them, it goes away.&#8221;</p>
<p>The Wall Street Journal <a target="_blank" href="http://online.wsj.com/article/SB10001424127887323949904578539721744479456.html"   class="external" rel="nofollow">reported</a> in June that due to what is dubbed a <a target="_blank" href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/tinabaker/2011/09/19/pre-startup-founder-agreements/"   class="external" rel="nofollow">founders&#8217; agreement</a>, &#8220;a sale of the newspapers could invalidate long-standing arrangements under which Tribune&#8217;s papers sell local ads on behalf of the websites, boosting the papers&#8217; digital ad revenue.&#8221;</p>
<p>Without the current revenue stream provided to the newspapers by CareerBuilders.com and Career Ventures LLC, the newspapers are unsustainable, the source said to The Daily Caller.</p>
<p>&#8220;The founders&#8217; agreement is the problem,&#8221; said the source.</p>
<p>Since Peter Liguori, former Discovery Communications chief operating officer, <a target="_blank" href="http://articles.chicagotribune.com/2013-01-17/business/chi-liguori-named-ceo-of-tribune-co-20130117_1_television-executive-peter-liguori-ceo-ross-levinsohn-chicago-tribune"   class="external" rel="nofollow">took</a> the helm as Tribune Company&#8217;s new CEO following its <a target="_blank" href="http://www.reuters.com/article/2012/12/31/us-tribune-bankruptcy-idUSBRE8BU02120121231"   class="external" rel="nofollow">emergence</a> from bankruptcy, he has pushed the company&#8217;s focus further towards broadcasting and cable.</p>
<p>The Tribune Company <a target="_blank" href="http://articles.chicagotribune.com/2013-07-01/business/chi-tribune-buying-local-tv-20130701_1_randy-michaels-tribune-studios-tribune-co"   class="external" rel="nofollow">announced</a> on July 1 a $2.73 billion all cash deal to buy Local TV LLC, which would add 19 stations in 16 markets to the company&#8217;s portfolio and make it the largest the nation&#8217;s largest commercial television station owner.</p>
<p>On July 10, Tribune Company <a target="_blank" href="http://corporate.tribune.com/pressroom/?p=5765"   class="external" rel="nofollow">announced</a> that it planned to spin off the publishing business into a separate company, Tribune Publishing Company, furthering Liguori&#8217;s vision to delve further into television media.</p>
<p>Chicago Tribune <a target="_blank" href="http://articles.chicagotribune.com/2013-07-10/business/chi-tribune-co-newspapers-20130710_1_tribune-co-tribune-studios-times-mirror-co"   class="external" rel="nofollow">wrote</a> that the move by the Tribune Company, however, &#8220;likely pushes back any potential sale of the publishing assets&#8221; until some time in 2014.</p>
<p>Koch Industries Chairman &amp; CEO Charles Koch had <a target="_blank" href="http://www.kansas.com/2013/07/09/2881836/charles-koch-launching-wichita.html"   class="external" rel="nofollow">told</a> the Wichita Eagle the previous day that the potential purchase of the newspapers was &#8220;not on the front burner&#8221; for his company but &#8220;still possible.&#8221;</p>
<p>Progressive activists opposed to the business and political activities of Koch Industries&#8217; leadership &#8212; Charles Koch and Executive Vice President David Koch &#8212; heavily <a target="_blank" href="http://articles.latimes.com/2013/may/29/business/la-fi-mo-activists-rally-outside-la-times-building-to-protest-potential-sale-to-koch-brothers-20130529"   class="external" rel="nofollow">dreaded</a> the potential purchase and organized protests against the business deal.</p>
<p>The Hartford City Council also <a target="_blank" href="http://courantblogs.com/cityline/council-passes-resolution-opposing-koch-brothers-takeover-of-tribune/"   class="external" rel="nofollow">passed</a> a resolution on August 12 opposing what its progressive members feared as a takeover by the Kochs&#8217; of Tribune, which owns the local Hartford Courant.</p>
<p>The resolution, introduced on May 28, was raised by members of the council&#8217;s Working Families Party,  whose political affiliations have been traced to organizations such as defunct Association of Community Organizations for Reform Now (ACORN) and the Service Employees International Union (SEIU).</p>
<p>When the Tribune Company announced the spin off of the newspapers, Kochs&#8217; critics speculated whether the move was meant to deter their ideological foes from purchasing a media property.</p>
<p>But a source with knowledge of the proceedings between the two companies told The Daily Caller that the decision to end the negotiations came from a recognition that the deal would be unfavorable for both parties involved.</p>
<p>Another source confirmed that Koch Industries is still interested in purchasing media properties. Koch Industries declined the Daily Caller&#8217;s request for comment.</p>
<p>Tribune Company spokesman Gary Weitman declined the Daily Caller&#8217;s request for comment, stating, &#8220;As a policy matter we do not comment on speculation about the company or its businesses.&#8221;</p>
<p><em>Correction: Peter Liguori was the former <em>chief operating officer of</em> Discovery Communications, not the Disney Communications as previously reported. </em></p>
