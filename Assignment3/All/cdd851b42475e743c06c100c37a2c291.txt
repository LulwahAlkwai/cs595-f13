<p class="bwalignc">

<p>    <location value="LU/us.wa.seatle" idsrc="xmltag.org">SEATTLE</location>--(BUSINESS WIRE)--Sep. 3, 2013--
      (NASDAQ:AMZN)—Amazon today introduced Kindle MatchBook, a new benefit
      that gives customers the option to buy—for <money>$2.99</money>, <money>$1.99</money>, <money>$0.99</money>, or
      free—the Kindle edition of print books they have purchased new from
      <org value="NASDAQ-NMS:AMZN" idsrc="xmltag.org">Amazon</org>. Print purchases all the way back to 1995—when <org value="NASDAQ-NMS:AMZN" idsrc="xmltag.org">Amazon</org> first
      opened its online bookstore—will qualify once a publisher enrolls a
      title in Kindle MatchBook. Over 10,000 books will already be available
      when Kindle MatchBook launches in October, including best sellers like<i>
      I Know This Much Is True </i>by <person>Wally Lamb</person>, <i>The Art of Racing in the
      Rain</i> by <person>Garth Stein</person>, <i>The Thorn Birds</i> by <person>Colleen McCullough</person>, <i>A
      Prayer for <person>Owen Meany</person></i> by <person>John Irving</person> and <i>The Hangman’s Daughter</i>
      by Oliver Pötzsch, with many more titles to be added over time.
      Customers can learn more by visiting <a href="http://cts.businesswire.com/ct/CT?id=smartlink&url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.amazon.com%2Fkindlematchbook&esheet=50700599&newsitemid=20130903005969&lan=en-US&anchor=www.amazon.com%2Fkindlematchbook&index=1&md5=bcdfbb10f40d97963642a7bbb1660a1c">www.amazon.com/kindlematchbook</a>.
    </p>
<p>
      “If you logged onto your CompuServe account during the Clinton
      administration and bought a book like <i>Men Are from Mars, Women Are
      from Venus</i> from <org value="NASDAQ-NMS:AMZN" idsrc="xmltag.org">Amazon</org>, Kindle MatchBook now makes it possible for
      that purchase—18 years later—to be added to your Kindle library at a
      very low cost,” said <person>Russ Grandinetti</person>, Vice President of Kindle Content.
      “In addition to being a great new benefit for customers, this is an easy
      choice for publishers and authors who will now be able to earn more from
      each book they publish.”
    </p>
<p>
      Bundling print and digital has been one of the most requested features
      from customers. With Kindle MatchBook, they can keep their favorite book
      on their shelf, and have a copy in their digital library for reading,
      perhaps re-reading it with features like X-Ray and Popular Highlights.
    </p>
<p>
      “I love this idea. It’s simple, brilliant, and good for everybody,” said
      best-selling author <person>Marcus Sakey</person>. “I love to have print books on my
      shelf, but I love reading my Kindle on the go, and there are plenty
      of titles I’d like both ways. It’s ridiculous to ask readers to pay full
      retail twice for the same book.”
    </p>
<p>
      Kindle MatchBook features include:
    </p>
<ul>
      <li class="bwlistitemmargb">
        <b>Kindle editions at a great price:</b> <org value="NASDAQ-NMS:AMZN" idsrc="xmltag.org">Amazon</org> customers who purchase
        or have purchased qualifying print books can get the Kindle edition
        for prices that are typically <money>$2.99</money>, <money>$1.99</money>, <money>$0.99</money>, or free.
      </li>
      <li class="bwlistitemmargb">
        <b>For book purchases dating back to 1995:</b> Print purchases all the
        way back to 1995—when <org value="NASDAQ-NMS:AMZN" idsrc="xmltag.org">Amazon</org> first opened its online bookstore—will
        qualify once a publisher enrolls a title in Kindle MatchBook.
      </li>
      <li class="bwlistitemmargb">
        <b>Easy discovery:</b> Readers can easily look up their entire print
        book order history to discover which of their past purchases are
        enrolled in Kindle MatchBook.
      </li>
      <li class="bwlistitemmargb">
        <b>Popular Kindle-only features:</b> As with regular purchases, Kindle
        MatchBook titles have unique features such as Whispersync, Popular
        Highlights, and X-Ray.
      </li>
      <li class="bwlistitemmargb">
        <b>Read anywhere capabilities: </b>In addition to Amazon’s
        best-selling Kindle devices, customers can download a free Kindle
        reading app for iPhone, iPad, Android tablets and phones, PC or Mac
        and start building their Kindle library today.
      </li>
    </ul>
<p>

      Crichton</person>, <person>Blake Crouch</person>, <person>James Rollins</person>, <person>Jodi Picoult</person>, <person>Neil Gaiman</person>, <person>Marcus
      Sakey</person>, <person>Wally Lamb</person>, <person>Jo Nesbo</person>, <person>Neal Stephenson</person>, and <person>J.A. Jance</person>, among
