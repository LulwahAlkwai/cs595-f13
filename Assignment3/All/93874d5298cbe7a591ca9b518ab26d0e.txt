        <div class="entry">
            <header>
                <h2>
                     <a href="http://scitechconnect.elsevier.com/category/chemistry/" title="Chemistry">Chemistry</a>,  <a href="http://scitechconnect.elsevier.com/category/earth-environmental-science/" title="Earth &amp; Environmental Science">Earth &amp; Environmental Science</a>                </h2>
                <h1>Reference Modules: Changing the Way We Do Research</h1>
                <small>
                    <span class="autor">By:  <a href="http://scitechconnect.elsevier.com/author/michelle-mcmahon/" title="Posts by Michelle McMahon" rel="author">Michelle McMahon</a></span>, Posted on: <time> September 16, 2013</time>
                                    </small>
                <div class="comments-count"><a href="#comments">Comments: 0</a></div>
            </header>
            <div class="post">
                
                
                <p><a href="http://www.sciencedirect.com/science/referenceworks/9780124095472?utm_source=SciTech+Connect&amp;utm_medium=BlogPressRelease&amp;utm_campaign=ChemRefMod" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.sciencedirect.com/science/referenceworks/9780124095472?utm_source=SciTech+Connect&amp;utm_medium=BlogPressRelease&amp;utm_campaign=ChemRefMod']);" target="_blank"><img class="alignleft" alt="Chemistry, Molecular sciences and Engineering Module on ScienceDirect" src="http://elsevierconnect.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Chemistry-Reference-Modules-logo.jpg" width="160" height="160" /></a>Scientific and technological research is becoming increasingly interdisciplinary, placing higher demands on researchers to get up to speed quickly on topics outside of their expertise. Researchers typically rely on reference works for such foundational content, yet publishers are challenged to provide that content in a way that fulfills the real needs those researchers have for currency.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.sciencedirect.com/science/referenceworks/9780124095489?utm_source=SciTech+Connect&amp;utm_medium=BlogPressRelease&amp;utm_campaign=EESRefMod" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.sciencedirect.com/science/referenceworks/9780124095489?utm_source=SciTech+Connect&amp;utm_medium=BlogPressRelease&amp;utm_campaign=EESRefMod']);" target="_blank"><img class="alignright" alt="Earth Systems and Environmental Sciences Module on ScienceDirect" src="http://elsevierconnect.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/ESES-Reference-Modules-logo.jpg" width="160" height="160" /></a></p>
<p>The key challenge is that traditional reference works are published every few years and their content can be very out-of-date.</p>
<p>Other common starting points for research are traditional search engines or online wikis, though each has its limitations. Search engines compile relevant <i>and</i> irrelevant results, making it hard to find the useful content. Online wikis lack rigorous review from subject-matter experts and are often not trustworthy or cited.</p>
<p>To address this need, Elsevier employees aimed to merge the reliability of traditional reference works with the regular updates an online solution could offer. They developed <a href="http://info.sciencedirect.com/ReferenceModules" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://info.sciencedirect.com/ReferenceModules']);">Elsevier Reference Modules on ScienceDirect</a>, which combine thousands of related reference-work articles into a single source of information that is continuously updated by experts. They unlock foundational content that has yet to be updated in print, giving researchers access to updated reference content as science progresses.</p>
<p>The first modules available to researchers are <a href="http://www.sciencedirect.com/science/referenceworks/9780124095472" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.sciencedirect.com/science/referenceworks/9780124095472']);">Chemistry, Molecular Sciences and Chemical Engineering</a> and <a href="http://www.sciencedirect.com/science/referenceworks/9780124095489" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.sciencedirect.com/science/referenceworks/9780124095489']);">Earth Systems and Environmental Sciences</a>.</p>
<p>Reference Modules, which <a href="http://www.elsevier.com/about/press-releases/science-and-technology/elsevier-reference-modules-on-sciencedirect-unlock-current-foundational-content"  target="_blank">launched</a> today, organize reference articles from across interdisciplinary scientific fields. The modules feature advanced search and browse tools. They are designed to be a trustworthy, citable reference that quickly gets researchers up to speed and connects them to more advanced content.</p>
<div style="text-align: center;"><iframe src="//www.youtube.com/embed/Z60Gk8E2IzI" height="315" width="420" allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0"></iframe></div>
<p>Most researchers are under increasing pressure to win funding and obtain valuable research outcomes in shorter amounts of time. And as research becomes more interdisciplinary, they must explore topics outside their areas of expertise.</p>
<p><strong>Elsevier Reference Module at a glance</strong></p>
<p><a href="http://scitechconnect.elsevier.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Ref-Mods-at-a-glance-box1.jpg" ><img class=" wp-image-2339 alignright" alt="Ref Mods at a glance box" src="http://scitechconnect.elsevier.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Ref-Mods-at-a-glance-box1.jpg" width="295" height="192" /></a>“I am being asked to stretch myself further and wider into fields that are not my immediate expertise,” said <a href="http://www.science.oregonstate.edu/~lajthak/about.php" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.science.oregonstate.edu/~lajthak/about.php']);">Dr. Kate Lajtha</a>, Reference Modules Subject Editor and Director of the <a href="http://envsci.science.oregonstate.edu/%E2%80%8E" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://envsci.science.oregonstate.edu/%E2%80%8E']);">Environmental Sciences Program at Oregon State University</a>. “(With Reference Modules), I can read a general overview of a new area, and then follow trails into more and more specific chapters as my research demands.”</p>
<p>The reference work articles are kept current by experts as science progresses. To show researchers just how current they are, there is a time stamp on each article indicating the date of last review or update. In the past, updated reference work content would have been unavailable to researchers until the typical five-to-seven year publishing cycle was complete.</p>
<p><strong>Learning from users</strong></p>
<div class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 125px"><a href="http://www.linkedin.com/in/corypolonetsky" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.linkedin.com/in/corypolonetsky']);" target="_blank"><img class="  " alt="Cory Polonetsky" src="http://elsevierconnect.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Cory-Polonetsky.png" width="115" height="159" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">Cory Polonetsky</p></div>
<p><a href="http://www.linkedin.com/in/corypolonetsky" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.linkedin.com/in/corypolonetsky']);">Cory Polonetsky</a>, head of marketing for the launch of Reference Modules, spent three days last week with dozens of chemists discussing their research needs at the <a href="http://www.acs.org/content/acs/en/meetings/fall-2013.html" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.acs.org/content/acs/en/meetings/fall-2013.html']);">American Chemical Society’s exposition</a> in Indianapolis.</p>
<p>Polonetsky noted some of the key challenges faced by researchers, including the chemists he met at the exposition: “Many struggle to determine whether an article they find is still ‘good science,’ ” he said. “Others worry that the content they purchase will be quickly out of date.”</p>
<p>He learned that those who teach often cringe when their students use online wikis for research, sacrificing expert review for ease of use. “Researchers want a trustworthy reference work that is comprehensive but easy to use and never gets old,” he said.</p>
<p>Several chemists at the conference told Polonetsky that Reference Modules will solve these problems. He thought Reference Modules were well-received at the exposition because “researchers value solutions that save time, can be cited, lead to significant research outcomes  and help them secure funding.”</p>
<p><strong>A sea change in publishing</strong></p>
<p>To publish this new type of foundational content, the team at Elsevier was required to completely change their approach.</p>
<div class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 114px"><a href="http://uk.linkedin.com/pub/lisa-tickner/5/292/a1a" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://uk.linkedin.com/pub/lisa-tickner/5/292/a1a']);" target="_blank"><img class=" " alt="Lisa Tickner" src="http://elsevierconnect.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Lisa-Tickner.jpg" width="104" height="141" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">Lisa Tickner</p></div>
<p>“It’s a massive change,” said <a href="http://uk.linkedin.com/pub/lisa-tickner/5/292/a1a" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://uk.linkedin.com/pub/lisa-tickner/5/292/a1a']);">Lisa Tickner</a>, Director for Continuity Publishing at Elsevier. “We’re leading the way in a new approach to publishing book content. We’ve been publishing electronic reference works for a while now, but it’s still been the case that you publish an e-book, wait a few years and then do another edition.”</p>
<p>She said this is Elsevier’s “first foray into modular publishing and continuous updating” and it took a lot of hard work and creative problem solving by many teams within Elsevier to bring Reference Modules to market.</p>
<p>To make Reference Modules a continuously-updated product on the <a href="http://www.sciencedirect.com/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.sciencedirect.com/']);">ScienceDirect</a> platform, they needed a completely different workflow than the one used to develop traditional reference books. This change presented new technical and production challenges.</p>
<p>The platform team had to figure out the right presentation of content and navigation on ScienceDirect, a full-text scientific database of more than 11 million journal articles and book chapters. There was also the challenge of changing the production workflow to handle continuously updating content at a rapid pace.</p>
<p>Meanwhile, the Continuity Publishing editorial team – the group that oversees the content in Elsevier reference works – completely revised the way they create, review and publish foundational content.</p>
<p><strong>Trustworthy</strong></p>
<p>To ensure the content in Reference Modules is trustworthy, they built editorial boards – teams of accomplished subject-matter experts – to select, organize and the review the content in each of the modules. There is an editorial board for each of the Reference Modules led by well-respected researchers:</p>
<p><a href="http://mcbim.lic.leidenuniv.nl/people/Reedijk" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://mcbim.lic.leidenuniv.nl/people/Reedijk']);" target="_blank"><img class="      alignleft" style="font-size: 13px; line-height: 19px;" alt="Jan Reedijk, PhD" src="http://elsevierconnect.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Jan-Reedijk-PhD-150x150.jpg" width="120" height="120" /></a></p>
<p><a href="http://pure.rhul.ac.uk/portal/en/persons/scott-elias_da6e306a-f31c-40ad-9ea8-89aa74bba587.html" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://pure.rhul.ac.uk/portal/en/persons/scott-elias_da6e306a-f31c-40ad-9ea8-89aa74bba587.html']);" target="_blank"><img class="  alignright" alt="Scott A. Elias, PhD" src="http://elsevierconnect.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/Scott-A.-Elias-PhD-150x150.jpg" width="120" height="120" /></a></p>
<p><a href="http://mcbim.lic.leidenuniv.nl/people/Reedijk" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://mcbim.lic.leidenuniv.nl/people/Reedijk']);">Dr. Jan Reedijk</a>, Emeritus Professor at <a href="http://leiden.edu/" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://leiden.edu/']);">Leiden University</a> in the Netherlands, is Editor-in-Chief for Chemistry, Molecular Sciences and Chemical Engineering.</p>
<p><a href="http://pure.rhul.ac.uk/portal/en/persons/scott-elias_da6e306a-f31c-40ad-9ea8-89aa74bba587.html" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://pure.rhul.ac.uk/portal/en/persons/scott-elias_da6e306a-f31c-40ad-9ea8-89aa74bba587.html']);">Dr. Scott Elias</a>, Professor of Quaternary Science at <a href="http://www.rhul.ac.uk/home.aspx" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://www.rhul.ac.uk/home.aspx']);">Royal Holloway, University of London</a>, is Editor-in-Chief for Earth Systems and Environmental Sciences.</p>
<p>These editorial boards organized content from Elsevier reference works into intuitive, interdisciplinary subject hierarchies. Once the content was sorted into these hierarchies, the editorial boards reviewed all the content for currency. The articles were either confirmed to be current, updated, or removed and replaced. This is the time stamping process, which assures researchers that they are using the most current information to inform their research.</p>
<p>With the first Reference Modules available, the editorial and production teams can take a breather – but not for long. Reference Modules are “living products,” as Tickner noted, and the work is never truly finished. The work of continuous updating goes on for the first two modules, while the team prepares to build new modules in the coming year.</p>
