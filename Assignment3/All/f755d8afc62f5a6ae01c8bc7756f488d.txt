<div id="item32542440" class="journal-entry">

  <div class="journal-entry-float-day"><span class="name">Sunday</span></div><div class="journal-entry-float-date"><span class="month">Jan</span><span class="date">13</span><span class="year">2013</span></div>
  
  
  
  <div class="journal-entry-text">
  
      
      <h2 class="title">
    
        
    
        <a class="journal-entry-navigation-current" href="/events/rationally-speaking-podcast-live-with-guest-ben-goldacre.html">Rationally Speaking Podcast Live: with Guest Ben&nbsp;Goldacre</a>  
     
         
    
      </h2>
      
      

    
  
      
      <div class="body">
  
        
        
        
          <p style="text-align: center;"><strong><span class="full-image-block ssNonEditable"><img src="/storage/images/events/RS_Live_web.png?__SQUARESPACE_CACHEVERSION=1348335543437" alt="" /></span></strong></p>
<p style="text-align: left;"><strong><span style="font-size: 120%;">When: Thursday February 21 - 7:00 PM</span></strong></p>
<p><strong><span style="font-size: 120%;">Where: University Settlement, 184 Eldridge Street (<a href="https://maps.google.com/maps?q=184+Eldridge+Street,+New+York,+NY&amp;hl=en&amp;sll=40.776066,-74.048314&amp;sspn=0.453937,1.056747&amp;oq=184+Eldridge+Street&amp;gl=us&amp;hnear=184+Eldridge+St,+New+York,+10002&amp;t=m&amp;z=17">map</a>)</span><br /></strong></p>
<p><strong><span style="font-size: 120%;">Free and open to the public!</span></strong></p>
<p><span class="full-image-float-left ssNonEditable"><span><img src="/storage/images/events/badpharma.jpg?__SQUARESPACE_CACHEVERSION=1358135767575" alt="" /></span></span>Join us first for a special lecture by Dr. Benjamin Goldacre about his new book, <em>Bad Pharma: How Drug Companies Mislead Doctors and Harm Patients</em>, and then Dr. Goldacre will be our special guest on a live taping of the Rationally Speaking podcast.</p>
<p>------------------</p>
<p><em>We like to imagine that medicine is based on evidence and the results of  fair tests. In reality, those tests are often profoundly flawed. We  like to imagine that doctors are familiar with the research literature  about a drug, when in reality much of the research is hidden from them  by drug companies. We like to imagine that doctors are impartially  educated, when in reality much of their education is funded by the  pharmaceutical industry. We like to imagine that regulators let only  effective drugs onto the market, when in reality they approve useless  drugs, with data on side effects casually withheld from doctors and  patients.</em></p>
<p><em>All these problems have been shielded from public  scrutiny because they&rsquo;re too complex to capture in a sound bite. But Ben  Goldacre shows that the true scale of this murderous disaster fully  reveals itself only when the details are untangled. He believes we  should all be able to understand precisely how data manipulation works  and how research misconduct on a global scale affects us. </em></p>
<p><em>With Goldacre&rsquo;s characteristic flair and a forensic attention to detail, Bad Pharma reveals  a shockingly broken system and calls for something to be done. This is  the pharmaceutical industry as it has never been seen before.</em></p>
