	<div class="article-body article-text">
	
		<p><div class="video-wsj-container" id="post-E5D4C140-34E1-437A-9516-840A0C08667D" style="width:640px; height:360px;"><div class="video-wsj" id="E5D4C140-34E1-437A-9516-840A0C08667D" style="width:640px; height:360px;"></div></div></p>
<p>You&#8217;ve seen this phone before. Sure, it has a new name: iPhone 5c. And its plastic body comes in different colors. It also has an attractive price when tied to a wireless contract.</p>
<p>But the 5c is really a slightly improved iPhone 5, which Apple is no longer offering.</p>
<p>This doesn&#8217;t make it a bad phone. Quite the contrary: I&#8217;ve been using the new iPhone 5c for the past five days and it&#8217;s a solid premium phone. Its hardware improvements include longer battery life, better cellular-data capabilities and a better front-facing camera, for FaceTime and &#8220;selfies,&#8221; or photos you take of yourself. </p>
<p>I like the candy-colored, tough plastic shell, as I am not a delicate flower and my phone shouldn&#8217;t be, either.</p>
<p><div id="attachment_359013" class="wp-caption alignright" style="width: 337px"><a href="http://i0.wp.com/allthingsd.com/files/2013/09/iPhone5c-allcolors.jpg"><img src="http://i1.wp.com/allthingsd.com/files/2013/09/iPhone5c-allcolors-327x285.jpg?resize=327%2C285" alt="The 5C iPhone comes in green, blue, yellow and pink, as well as white." class="size-medium wp-image-359013" data-recalc-dims="1" /></a><p class="wp-caption-text">The 5c iPhone comes in green, blue, yellow and pink, as well as white (Credit: Apple).</p></div></p>
<p>Many of the improvements in the iPhone 5c are related to the phone&#8217;s upgraded mobile software, which isn&#8217;t exclusive to the 5c. It includes a redesigned interface, new multitasking options, a supposedly smarter Siri and a better iTunes. Earlier versions of iPhone &#8212; iPhone 4 and up &#8212; and newer iPads can run this updated software, as well. </p>
<p>But the iPhone 5c, positioned as one of two new iPhones in Apple&#8217;s product line, probably won&#8217;t satisfy tech-savvy consumers who need the latest and greatest hardware. Those people will probably turn their eyes toward the <a href="http://allthingsd.com/20130917/a-new-touch-for-iphone/">iPhone 5s</a>, which has a powerful 64-bit processor and new fingerprint-recognition technology that lets you unlock the phone with a tap of your finger. </p>
<p>If you want a new iPhone but don&#8217;t want to splurge on the 5s, or are a first-timer lured in by the contract price, then the 5c might be a good choice.</p>
<p>The iPhone 5c starts at $99 with a two-year contract from AT&#038;T and Verizon. Sprint is offering the phone free of charge to customers who switch over from another carrier. T-Mobile is offering the phone for $0 down with one of its two-year payment plans, totaling $528 without a contract. </p>
<p>The unlocked price is $549 and $649 for the 16 gigabyte and 32GB models, respectively. </p>
<p>The 5c is made of a hard-coated polycarbonate with steel reinforcement inside the body. It comes in white, watermelon pink, bright yellow, electric blue and lime green &#8212; a sort of iPod-ification of the iPhone. Out of the box, the background wallpaper and lock screen match the exterior. </p>
<p>The iPhone 5c has the same A6 processor and the same four-inch Retina display as the iPhone 5. Colors on the 5c&#8217;s display look warmer. </p>
<p>It&#8217;s just slightly thicker and heavier than the iPhone 5. And while the 5c isn&#8217;t as refined-looking as the iPhone 5 or the new 5s, it isn&#8217;t a tacky plastic phone, either. I&#8217;ve tested plastic phones before, including the Samsung Galaxy S4 and the new Moto X from Motorola, and this phone feels more solid in the hand. Call quality was good.</p>
<p>Apple has put a larger battery in the 5c, though the company has declined to say exactly how much bigger it is. Since the display and processor remain the same, a user should be able to get some extra juice with this phone. </p>
<p>This past weekend I used both the 5c and my own iPhone 5 at the same time, with the display on both set to about 75 percent of full brightness and their batteries fully charged. I ran the same apps, including maps apps, browsed through both Safari browsers and made phone calls on both phones. When my iPhone 5 died on Saturday night, the 5c had 17 percent battery power left.</p>
<p>At last week&#8217;s launch event for the 5c and 5s, Apple stressed the addition of more LTE bands to its phone lineup. </p>
<p>What does this mean? Basically, there isn&#8217;t one LTE standard &#8212; different carriers offer many types of LTE networks around the world. The previous iPhone 5 was compatible with up to five bands. A couple of phones claim eight. Now, the iPhone 5c and 5s are compatible with up to 13 LTE bands. </p>
<p>So in France, the iPhone can now run on Orange&#8217;s network at LTE speeds. The same goes for Vodafone in the U.K. and Germany. That means travelers can pop in a local prepaid SIM card in more countries and pull down LTE speeds, currently up to 100 megabits per second. </p>
<p>The camera technology in the 5c has improved a tiny bit. The front-facing camera promises larger pixels, capturing slightly better images than the front-facing camera of the iPhone 5. </p>
<p>IOS 7 also includes an update to the iPhone&#8217;s camera software. The camera app feels more professional and has built-in filters &#8212; finally bringing the iPhone up to speed with other premium smartphone cameras. </p>
<p>Keep in mind, however, the iPhone 5c&#8217;s rear-facing camera has the same 8-megapixel sensor as the iPhone 5 and captures the same 1080p HD video. </p>
<p>While the 5c looks and feels very familiar, it&#8217;s still a good phone and an improvement over the 5. But its improvements are evolutionary, not revolutionary.</p>
