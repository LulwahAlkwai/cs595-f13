                            <span class="lines">
                                &#8220;Artisanal&#8221; Publishing                            </span>
                        </h2>
                 </div> <!-- end sixteen columns -->

                 <div class="clear"></div>

            <p><a href="http://apethebook.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/APE_Cover_20130118.jpg" ><img src="http://apethebook.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/01/APE_Cover_20130118.jpg" alt="" title="Cover400x639" width="187" height="300" class="alignright size-medium wp-image-279" style="border: 4px solid white; margin-top: 25px;"/></a></p>
<p>Welcome to the <em>APE: Author, Publisher, Entrepreneur—How to Publish a Book</em> website. This site contains all the information you need to buy, review, and use <em>APE</em>.</p>
<p>In 2011 the publisher of Guy Kawasaki’s <em>New York Times</em> bestseller, <em>Enchantment</em>, could not fill an order for 500 ebook copies of the book. Because of this experience, Guy self-published his next book, <em>What the Plus!</em> and learned first-hand that self-publishing is a complex, confusing, and idiosyncratic process. As Steve Jobs said, “There must be a better way.”</p>
<p>With Shawn Welch, a tech wizard, Guy wrote <em>APE</em> to help people take control of their writing careers by publishing their books. The thesis of <em>APE</em> is simple but powerful: When a self-publisher successfully fills three roles—author, publisher and entrepreneur—the potential benefits are greater than with traditional publishing.</p>
<p>Guy and Shawn call this “artisanal publishing.”</p>
<p>Artisanal publishing features writers who love their craft, and who control every aspect of the process from beginning to end. In this new approach, writers are no longer at the mercy of large, traditional publishers, and readers will have more books to read.</p>
<p><em>APE</em> is 300 pages of tactical and practical inspiration. People who want a hype-filled, get-rich-quick book should look elsewhere. On the other hand, if you want a comprehensive and realistic guide to self-publishing, <em>APE</em> is for you.</p>
