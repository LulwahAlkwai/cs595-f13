#LeapInto Google Earth

     * [14]22/04/13
     * [15]22 April, 2013
     * [16]Comments
     *
     * [17]Tweet
     *

   With Google Earth and the Leap Motion Controller, you can soar above
   the globe and witness all the sights that our planet has to offer.
   Explore your favorite place on Earth, or go to the Moon. Dive into
   distant mountains, deserts, and rainforests. Leap into the world's
   greatest cities with the world's most accurate 3D motion control
   technology. The world is at your fingertips.

   [18]Google Earth with the Leap Motion Controller

   When you're ready, pick a spot to fly around, take a video of your
   hands controlling Google Earth, post it, and use the hashtag #LeapInto
   __ (insert location). Example: "Here's my #LeapInto San Francisco,
   using @LeapMotion." We have a [19]YouTube Playlist to collect and share
   your videos.

How to get started

  Mac Users

     * Download and install the [20]Google Earth desktop client, and open
       the application. (If you already have Google Earth, make sure that
       it's been updated to the latest version.)
     * Click on Google Earth in the navigation bar, then Preferences.

   image
     * Click on the Navigation tab, and in the Non-mouse
       Controller section, check the Enable Controller box.

   image
     * Click Apply and OK.
     * Turn on the Leap Motion Controller and begin exploring.

  Windows Users

     * Download and install the [21]Google Earth desktop client, and open
       the application. (If you already have Google Earth, make sure that
       it's been updated to the latest version.)
     * Click on Tools in the menu bar, then Options.

   image
     * Click on the Navigation tab, and in the Non-mouse Controller
       section, check the Enable Controller box.

   image
     * Click Apply and OK.
     * Turn on the Leap Motion Controller and begin exploring.

  About Google Earth

   Since it launched in 2005, more than one billion people have downloaded
   Google Earth. Over that time, it has redefined how we see our planet -
   allowing us to take it in with a single glance, before panning across
   and zooming into its vast landscapes and tiny cities. It helps us
   appreciate the unimaginable scale and awesome beauty of our pale blue
   dot.
