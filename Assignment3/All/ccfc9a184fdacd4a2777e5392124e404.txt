												<h1 class="entry-title">More P v NP</h1>
		</header>

		<div class="entry-content">
			<p><span style="line-height: 1.714285714; font-size: 1rem;">Steve Cook&#8217;s </span><a style="line-height: 1.714285714; font-size: 1rem;" href="http://www.claymath.org/millennium/P_vs_NP/pvsnp.pdf">Official P versus NP problem Description</a><span style="line-height: 1.714285714; font-size: 1rem;">  for the </span><a style="line-height: 1.714285714; font-size: 1rem;" href="http://www.claymath.org/millennium/">Clay Mathematics Institute Millennium Prizes</a></p>
<p>Lance Fortnow&#8217;s September 2009 <em>Communications of the ACM</em> article <a href="http://cacm.acm.org/magazines/2009/9/38904-the-status-of-the-p-versus-np-problem/fulltext">The Status of the P versus NP Problem</a> that inspired this book.</p>
<p>Gödel&#8217;s 1956<a href="http://blog.computationalcomplexity.org/2006/04/kurt-gdel-1906-1978.html"> letter to von Neumann</a> where he essentially describes the P versus NP problem fifteen years before Cook&#8217;s <a href="http://www.chell.co.uk/media/product/_master/1/files/cook_complexity_of_theorem_proving_procedures_19712.pdf">seminal paper</a>.</p>
<p><strong>Blogs</strong></p>
<p><a href="http://blog.computationalcomplexity.org">Computational Complexity</a> (Lance Fortnow and Bill Gasarch)</p>
<p><a href="http://rjlipton.wordpress.com/">Gödel&#8217;s Lost Letter and P=NP</a> (Dick Lipton and Ken Regan)</p>
<p><strong>Books</strong></p>
<p><a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0716710455/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=0716710455&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=computation09-20">Computers and Intractability: A Guide to the Theory of NP-Completeness</a> by Michael Garey and David Johnson. Despite its age, still the best mathematical introduction and reference book on P versus NP and NP-complete problems.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0521122546?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=213733&amp;creative=393185&amp;creativeASIN=0521122546&amp;linkCode=shr&amp;tag=computation09-20">P, NP, and NP-Completeness: The Basics of Computational Complexity</a> by Oded Goldreich. A more technical but still accessible introduction to P versus NP and beyond.</p>
<p><span style="font-size: 1rem;"><a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0521199565?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=213733&amp;creative=393185&amp;creativeASIN=0521199565&amp;linkCode=shr&amp;tag=computation09-20&amp;=books&amp;qid=1363875631">Quantum Computing since Democritus</a> by Scott Aaronson. A fun romp through logic, complexity and  quantum computing. </span></p>
<p><a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/113318779X/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=113318779X&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=computation09-20">Introduction to the Theory of Computation</a> by Michael Sipser. An excellent undergraduate textbook on the basics of theoretical computer science and the P versus NP problem.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0521424267/ref=as_li_ss_tl?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=0521424267&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;tag=computation09-20">Computational Complexity: A Modern Approach</a> by Sanjeev Arora and Boaz Barak. A solid graduate text on P, NP and beyond.</p>
<p><strong>Clay Mathematics Institute Lecture on P versus NP by Michael Sipser</strong></p>
