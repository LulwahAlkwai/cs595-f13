                          make practical contributions to the more effective use 
                        of scientific and technical knowledge in federal decision-making.						The Revelle Fellowship is designed to 
                          provide a unique opportunity for an accomplished scientist 
                          to address global stewardship issues by applying his 
                          or her broad, multidisciplinary background toward solutions 
                        to  important societal problems, including:</p>
<ul>

							</li>
							<li>population						
							</li>

</p>

</ul>

	<p>
		
	<!-- END CONTENT --><strong><a name="overseas" id="overseas"></a>AAAS Overseas Fellowships at USAID Missions</strong> (Eligibility limited to current/alumni AAAS Fellows only)</p>
<p>Re-launched in 2011, the AAAS Overseas Fellowships  offer Fellows the experience of living and working overseas at a U.S. Embassy or US Agency for International Development (USAID) Mission , as well as the opportunity to expand both the service and professional development opportunities of the S&amp;T Policy Fellowship experience. AAAS supports Fellows to pursue international assignments via the Overseas Fellowships. AAAS Overseas Fellowships are currently available only at USAID Missions. Overseas Fellowships offer one- to two-year international assignments. </p>
<p> The Fellow's primary responsibility will be to help the Mission identify key opportunities to develop strategies and programs that contribute to development in science and technology (S&amp;T) and areas where S&amp;T can be harnessed to advance development in the host country and/or region.</p>
<p> This fellowship opportunity is limited to current and alumni AAAS Science &amp; Technology Policy Fellows. Fellows may have served in any program area. Additional criteria and the evaluation and placement processes are outlined in the <a href="/PDFs/Overseas_Fellowship_Guidelines_2012.pdf">AAAS Overseas Fellowship Guidelines</a>. You can also find more information in the <a href="/PDFs/Overseas_fellowships_FAQs.pdf" target="_blank">AAAS  Overseas Fellowships FAQs</a>.</p>
<p>You can learn more about how Fellows  contribute to USAID Missions in this short <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BZJ4MW8Al74&feature=youtu.be">video</a>.</p>
<p>Anticipated placement opportunities will  vary from year to year. For more information, please contact <a href="mailto:ckalyand@aaas.org">Chitra  Kalyandurg</a>,  program manager. </p>
<p><b><img src="/images_Fellows/Elements/Dot_grey.gif" alt="line" width="100%" height="1" vspace="5"></b></p>
	<p><strong><a name="GHD"></a>Global Health &amp; Development Fellowships</strong> (Eligibility limited to current/alumni AAAS Fellows only)</p>
<p>The AAAS S&amp;T Policy Fellowships have partnered with the Bill &amp; Melinda Gates Foundation to create the Global Health &amp; Development (GHD) Fellowships. This new fellowship opportunity places Fellows at the Gates Foundation in Seattle, Washington, for one year, with the potential to renew for a second year. </p>
<p> The GHD Fellowships provide opportunities for Fellows to learn from and contribute to the international development initiatives of the Foundation, by helping to identify and support strategies and programs in which science and technology can be harnessed to advance development in designated countries and/or regions. </p>
<p> This fellowship opportunity is open to current and alumni AAAS Science &amp; Technology Policy Fellows. Fellows may have served in any program area. Please refer to the <a href="/PDFs/2013-14_GHD_guidelines.pdf" target="_blank">AAAS Global Health &amp; Development Fellowship Guidelines</a> for additional information on eligibility criteria, the application process, and the program in general.</p>
	<p>If you have questions please contact <a href="mailto:%20srussell@aaas.org">Sage Russell</a>, associate director.
