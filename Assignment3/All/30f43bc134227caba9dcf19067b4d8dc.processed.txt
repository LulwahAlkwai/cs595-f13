The Molly Solution

   This week Bill and I are both at the [5]Dagstuhl Workshop on Algebraic
   Methods in Computation Complexity. I'll try to cover some of the talks
   and discussions on the blog and on Twitter.
   Called home last night and had the following conversation with my
   11-year old daughter.
   Molly: I was thinking about this P/NP problem. What do P and N stand
   for?
   Lance: You probably won't understand but P stands for "Polynomial-Time"
   and N stands for "Nondeterministic"
   Molly: Well, if P and N were random variables then P=NP if N equals
   one.
   An old joke, but my daughter had figured it out all on her own. So I
   answered her with the only response I could give.
   Lance: Or if P equals zero.
