Goodbye Old Friends

   We had two terrible losses this week. Not people but websites,
   [5]Intrade and [6]Google Reader.
   [7][Picture1.png]
   The corporate auditors for the real-money Irish prediction markets site
   Intrade [8]found improprieties with payments to the late founder John
   Delaney and have effectively shut down the site, probably for good. I
   never bet money on Intrade but they made their bet data easily
   available. I used Intrade data to power my [9]electoral markets map,
   possibly the most accurate predictor of elections, at least until Nate
   Silver came along. Even then our map updated in real time based on new
   information reflected in market prices, while Nate had to wait for poll
   data. I also used Intrade generated graphs of prediction market prices
   in dozens of talks I've given over the past decade. There are [10]other
   sites for prediction aggregation but painful to lose the granddaddy of
   them all.
   Google [11]announced it is closing down Google Reader, Google's
   newsreader on July 1. I find Reader invaluable for keeping up with the
   theory blogs, especially those that don't post that often (I'm looking
   at you [12]Scott and [13]Luca) as well as a various collection of other
   blogs and my daily Dilbert. Not to mention the 2241 people who
   subscribe to this blog on Google Reader. There are alternatives (I'm
   trying [14]Feedly) and I will be more vigilant on sharing posts on
   [15]Twitter and [16]Google+ but the real question is to why do fewer
   people use Google Reader. Is the flood of stuff on the Internet gotten
   so large we don't even try to keep up with it?
