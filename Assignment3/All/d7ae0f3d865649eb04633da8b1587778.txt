												<h1 class="entry-title">Errors</h1>
		</header>

		<div class="entry-content">
			<p>In Figure 3-2 (page 34), David and Helen should not be friends.</p>
<p>In chapter 2, I used the fictional name Milena Pavel for the Czech woman who settled the P versus NP problem. Pavel is the male form of the name &#8211; the female form is Pavlova. Whether Milena was married and took her husbands name or was the daughter whose father&#8217;s name was Pavel, she would have taken the name Pavlova.</p>
<p>Don&#8217;t try to solve the large Sudoku problem on page 61, there are two G&#8217;s on the top row.</p>
<p>On page 55, I give credit to Richard Karp for the reduction of Satisfiability to Clique. That reduction does first appear in Karp&#8217;s paper but in that paper Karp says &#8220;the reduction to CLIQUE is implicit in Cook&#8217;s paper, and was also known to Ray Reiter.&#8221;</p>
<p>On page 58, the book states &#8220;It took Donald Knuth about four decades to finish volume 4.&#8221; Volume 4A of <a href="http://www-cs-faculty.stanford.edu/~uno/taocp.html">The Art of Computer Programming</a> was published in 2011 but the rest of Volume 4 is still a work in progress.</p>
<p>In the map of Armenia, page 94, Shirak should have the same color as Armavir.</p>
<p>On page 83 the book places Novosibirsk west of Moscow when it is, in fact, east of Moscow.</p>
<p>The &#8220;propositions&#8221; in the parse tree on page 75 of &#8220;Golden Ticket&#8221; should be &#8220;prepositions&#8221;.</p>
<p><strong>Typos</strong></p>
<p>First sentence of the section on &#8220;The Hand&#8221;, page 5, &#8220;every&#8221; should be &#8220;ever&#8221;.</p>
<p>On Page 58, Chapter 4, the 2nd sentence in the paragraph starting with &#8220;Knuth was not entirely happy&#8230;&#8221;, it should be &#8220;English word&#8221; Knuth tried to find, not &#8220;English world&#8221;.</p>
<p>On Page 75, first paragraph, &#8220;difficultly&#8221; should be &#8220;difficulty&#8221;.</p>
<p>On page 82, since it is a sequence of digits not a number there shouldn&#8217;t be any commas (or a comma after each digit).</p>
<p>On page 155 the book talks about the &#8220;beautiful world&#8221; in chapter 1, but the description of that world is in chapter 2.</p>
<p>On page 156, &#8220;buzz in that fraction of a section&#8221;, &#8220;section&#8221; should be &#8220;second&#8221;.</p>
<p>On page 164, <span style="font-size: 1rem;">Pálvölgyi </span><span style="line-height: 1.714285714; font-size: 1rem;">Dömötör should be </span><span style="line-height: 1.714285714; font-size: 1rem;">Dömötör Pálvölgyi.</span></p>
<p>On page 168, the Gentry paper should be dated 2009 not 1979.</p>
<p>&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;&#8212;-</p>

<p>Please <a href="mailto:fortnow@cc.gatech.edu">email the author</a> if you find an error in the book.</p>
