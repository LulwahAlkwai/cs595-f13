<section class='article-content'>

<p>In the first half of 2013, <a href="http://mashable.com/category/yahoo/">Yahoo</a> received 12,444 government data requests, which is more than either <a href="http://mashable.com/category/facebook/">Facebook</a> and <a href="http://mashable.com/category/twitter/">Twitter</a> did. It also received more than <a href="http://mashable.com/category/google/">Google</a> did in the second half of 2012, the last period for which it reported its government requests.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.statista.com/topics/1001/google/chart/1443/number-of-govermental-data-requests-to-selected-tech-companies/" target="_blank">Statista</a> created this chart comparing the amount of government data requests received by four major tech companies. Though Microsoft publishes a transparency report, it quantifies its data in 12-month periods, so it couldn't be compared with the companies on this chart. </p>
<div class="see-also"><p>See also: <a href="http://mashable.com/2013/06/14/infographic-how-prism-might-work/" data-crackerjax="#post-slider">See How PRISM May Work â€” in This Infographic</a></p></div>
<p>This new data was made available by Yahoo, in its <a href="http://mashable.com/2013/09/06/yahoo-transparency-report/" data-crackerjax="#post-slider">first transparency report</a> published on Friday, and <a href="http://mashable.com/category/facebook/">Facebook</a>, in its <a href="http://mashable.com/2013/08/27/facebook-transparency-report/" data-crackerjax="#post-slider">first transparency report</a> published on Tuesday.</p>
<p><em><strong>UPDATE, September 10, 10:45 a.m.:</strong></em> It's important to note that Google and Twitter don't publish the number of national-security related requests along with the other requests coming from law-enforcement, as opposed to the other companies, which publish an aggregate number. </p>
