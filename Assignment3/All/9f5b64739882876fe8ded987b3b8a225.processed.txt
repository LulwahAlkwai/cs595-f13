How giant companies build their future by buying little ones

   By Harry McCracken Monday, Sept. 16, 2013

   When one major tech company plunks down billions for another--think
   Microsoft buying Nokia--everybody pays attention. The vast majority of
   deals, however, don't make headlines. They involve low-profile startups
   and purchase prices in the mere tens of millions of dollars. But that
   doesn't mean they're insignificant. In fact, they can tell you an awful
   lot about where the industry's best-known names are headed.

   In most cases, the acquirers don't care about the products they've
   bought. (To prove it, they often instantly shut them down, as Yahoo did
