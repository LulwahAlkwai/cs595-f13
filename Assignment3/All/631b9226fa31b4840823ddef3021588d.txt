                <h1 class="title" id="page-title">What Do Science Writers Do? </h1>
                        <div class="block block-system block-main block-system-main odd block-without-title" id="block-system-main">
  <div class="block-inner clearfix">
                
    <div class="content clearfix">
      <article class="node node-page node-published node-not-promoted node-not-sticky author-andrea odd clearfix" id="node-page-483">
            
  
  <div class="content clearfix">
    <div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even"> <h3>A Guide To Careers in Science Writing</h3>
<ul><li><a href="http://casw.org/casw/who-are-science-writers">Who Are Science Writers?</a></li>
<li><a href="http://casw.org/casw/what-do-science-writers-do"><strong>What Do Science Writers Do?</strong></a></li>
<li><a href="http://casw.org/casw/how-do-science-writers-get-their-stories">How Do Science Writers Get Their Stories?</a></li>
<li><a href="http://casw.org/casw/how-do-i-know-if-i-should-become-science-writer-and-if-i-have-what-it-takes">How Do I Know If I Should Become a Science Writer and If I Have What It Takes?</a></li>
<li><a href="http://casw.org/casw/how-much-money-do-science-writers-make">How Much Money Do Science Writers Make?</a></li>
<li><a href="http://casw.org/casw/how-do-i-get-started-science-writing">How Do I Get<strong> </strong>Started in Science Writing?</a></li>
<li><a href="http://casw.org/casw/references">References</a></li>
</ul><p> </p>
<p>With the rise of web journalism, science writers are becoming less  categorized by the media outlet they work for. More and more they are  becoming multimedia "backpack" journalists, not only bringing their  traditional notebooks to cover stories, but also audio recorders and  still and video cameras, and computers with which to tweet and blog.</p>
<p>For example, newspaper and magazine science journalists may write  news and feature articles for their print publications and also produce  blog posts, podcasts, interactive graphics and video for their websites.</p>
<p>Conversely, radio and TV science journalists, besides writing scripts  and turning them into audio and video segments, may also turn out text  articles for the web site.</p>
<p>Science writers may not even work for traditional outlets, but may be  independent bloggers or bloggers affiliated with the web sites of  magazines or other media. Much of a writer’s time may be spent using  social media tools to filter breaking science news and interact directly  with audiences.</p>
<p>Book authors produce the most in-depth science reporting, devoting  often years of work and hundreds of thousands of words to explaining  their topics. In addition to fulfilling the traditional work of an  author, they may also be involved in producing audio, video and  interactive content for ebooks and tablet apps.</p>
<p>Similarly, the work of science PIOs extends beyond text news releases  to video news releases, audio podcasts, blog posts and reports for  social media outlets (e.g. Facebook, Twitter) explaining research at  their institutions </p>
<p>News releases are produced in a journalistic style but commonly offer  only the perspective of the PIO’s employer. Such releases are also  frequently more detailed than a news article, since the releases must  contain information meant for a wide range of science writers and other  audiences. There are many types of news releases, including:</p>
<ul><li>The hard news release, which reports on a discovery published in  a scientific journal or discussed in a talk at a scientific conference</li>
<li>The feature release, which describes work in progress and is not generally tied to a newly published result</li>
<li>The backgrounder, a nuts-and-bolts history of a piece of research</li>
<li>The personal profile, which describes the scientist’s professional and private worlds.</li>
<li>The Q&amp;A, which offers a scientist's opinions and explanations in his/her own words</li>
<li>The media alert, which notifies journalists of a news conference or other event</li>
<li>The grant/gift announcement, which describes a new gift or grant, explaining the objectives of the new research</li>
<li>The award announcement, which announces an honor or prize given a scientist</li>
<li>The tipsheet, a collection of short items alerting journalists to work being presented at a conference or in a journal issue</li>
</ul><p>Besides news releases, science PIOs may also produce articles for institutional magazines, speeches and brochure copy.</p>
<p>PIOs also oversee distribution of their releases and other content to  the media and other audiences, handle media inquiries and organize and  conduct news conferences. They may also assist scientists in providing  background and opinion for journalists preparing stories on breaking  news with a science angle.</p>
<p>More broadly, they may manage communication strategies for  controversial issues from animal rights, to scientific misconduct, to  conflict of interest. They may also organize educational and background  conferences and courses for science journalists and manage  journalist-in-residence programs. And they may develop communication  training programs for their scientists.</p>
