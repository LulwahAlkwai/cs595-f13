First Mobile Phone Call Was Placed Exactly 40 Years Ago

   17.0k
   Shares
   [44]Share
   [45]Tweet
   [46]Share
   What's This?
   Marty_cooper
   [47]Stan-schroeder
   By Stan Schroeder2013-04-03 11:06:34 UTC

   On April 3, 1973 -- exactly 40 years from today -- Motorola employee
   Marty Cooper made the first mobile phone call.

   Marty used a Motorola DynaTAC to call Bell Labs (then a division of
   AT&T), [48]reportedly saying "I'm ringing you just to see if my call
   sounds good at your end."

   The device that Marty used to place the call was a prototype which
   would later become the Motorola DynaTAC 8000x. It was the first
   commercially available mobile phone, and despite its meager specs for
   today's standards -- it weighed 2.5 pounds and only had a one-line,
   text-only LED display -- it cost a whopping $3,995.

   SEE ALSO: [49]Cell-ebration! 40 Years of Cellphone History

   The DynaTAC phone can be seen in action in several Hollywood films,
   including "Wall Street," where Gordon Gekko uses it to place a call
   from the beach, and "American Psycho," with [50]Patrick Bateman using
   it to place a fake dinner reservation call.

   With today's phones being immeasurably [51]more powerful and a lot
   cheaper, there's one quality the mobile phone has lost -- exclusivity.
   If that's what you're after, well, you can always get yourself one of
   [52]these.
