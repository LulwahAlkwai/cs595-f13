						<div class="meta-title">
		<div class="cat-ttl-box" mobile style="background-color:#009fff;"></div><span class="cat-ttl" style="text-transform:uppercase; color:#009fff;"><a class="hu" href="http://bgr.com/mobile/">mobile</a></span>

					<span class="post-time">11:25 AM</span><p>During <a href="http://bgr.com/tag/apple">Apple&#8217;s</a> iPhone event this past Tuesday, a number of rival vendors took to Twitter in an attempt to have some fun at Apple&#8217;s expense. Unfortunately, most of them live in glass houses. Among the merry smartphone makers was Nokia, which <a href="https://twitter.com/nokia_uk/statuses/377483408043036672" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">poked fun as Apple&#8217;s colorful new iPhone 5c lineup</a> as it gets ready to <a href="http://bgr.com/2013/09/06/nokia-microsoft-sale-analysis-anssi/">offload the failed handset business it couldn&#8217;t figure out how to revive</a>, and <a href="http://bgr.com/tag/motorola">Motorola</a>, which made fun of the embedded fingerprint scanner on the <a href="http://bgr.com/tag/iphone-5s">iPhone 5s</a>. The problem with Motorola mocking Apple&#8217;s new fingerprint scanner, of course, is the fact that Motorola itself launched a smartphone with an embedded fingerprint reader back in 2011.<span id="more-1207098"></span></p>
<p>&#8220;Remember that one time you were stoked to give your fingerprints? Us either,&#8221; Motorola wrote in a tweet. The vendor, which managed an <a href="http://bgr.com/2013/07/18/google-earnings-analysis-q2-2013-motorola/">operating loss of more than $300 million last quarter</a>, then linked to its new promotional video, which promises that <a href="http://bgr.com/2013/09/09/morotola-moto-x-ad-video/">theÂ Moto X is the answer to the &#8220;lazy phone.&#8221;</a></p>
<blockquote class="twitter-tweet"><p>Remember that one time you were stoked to give your fingerprints? Us either. <a href="https://twitter.com/search?q=%23LazyPhone&amp;src=hash" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">#LazyPhone</a> <a href="http://t.co/ckuu7otnJM" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">http://t.co/ckuu7otnJM</a></p>
<p>â€” Motorola Mobility (@Motorola) <a href="https://twitter.com/Motorola/statuses/377506227967627264" rel="nofollow" target="_blank">September 10, 2013</a></p></blockquote>
<p>As <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/andygreenberg/2013/09/11/motorola-bashes-apples-iphone-fingerprint-reader-forgets-it-sold-one-first/" rel="nofollow" target="_blank"><em>Forbes</em> points out</a>, however, Motorola itself launched a smartphone with an embedded fingerprint scanner â€” and it wasn&#8217;t even that long ago.</p>
<p>The <a href="http://bgr.com/2011/02/09/motorola-atrix-4g-review/">Motorola Atrix 4G</a> featured an in-built print reader that was embedded in the phone&#8217;s unlock button. And <a href="http://bgr.com/2011/02/09/motorola-atrix-4g-review/">as we discussed in out Atrix 4G review back in February 2011</a>, the scanner served the exact same purpose as Apple&#8217;s fingerprint reader, allowing users to unlock the phone without having to enter a PIN code.</p>
<p>Oops.</p>
