	 <h1 class="storytitle" id="post-5013" itemprop="name"><a href="http://lemire.me/blog/archives/2013/02/20/what-kind-of-researcher-are-you/" rel="bookmark" itemprop="url"  title="Posted on February 20th, 2013">What kind of researcher are you?</a></h1>
	<div class="storycontent"   itemprop="articleBody">
		<ul>
<li><strong>The politician</strong>. He will get people to collaborate on joint projects.
<p>How to recognize: He knows everyone!</p>
<p>Pro: He makes things happen irrespective of the available funding! </p>
<p>Con: Sends you emails on a Saturday.
</li>
<li><strong>The manager</strong>. His job is primarily to seek funding and recruit students. He supervises the work, setting directions and reviewing results.
<p>How to recognize: Will often publish with many student authors. Will receive (or seek) generous funding. </p>
<p>Pro: A good manager can scale well. If you increase his funding, he will produce more results without necessarily sacrificing quality.</p>
<p>Con: When the manager runs out of funding, his productivity might collapse. He might not stay close to the research: a manager is rarely passionate about the product, as he focuses on the process.
</li>
<li><strong>The clueless</strong>. He does not know what he is doing. He does not understand the business.
<p>How to recognize: Will religiously follow trends without any kind of critical thinking. Might stay close to a manager. </p>
<p>Pro: Can be convinced to render useful services.</p>
<p>Con: Might inflate fads. Will typically produce work of little significance.
</li>
<li><strong>The dreamer</strong>. He has a vision. He might be a perfectionist.
<p>How to recognize: Might not publish very much. </p>
<p>Pro: Might just be the person to crack the hardest problem of your generation&#8230;</p>
<p>Con: &#8230; but might also produce nothing. Good at wasting funding.
</li>
<li><strong>The artisan</strong>. He builds research projects from the ground up. Routinely, he will have complete ownership over a research project. Extra funding might not increase his productivity.
<p>How to recognize: Will publish significant work alone.</p>
<p>Pro: He really cares about his research.</p>
<p>Con:  He might have a narrow focus.
</li>
<li><strong>The prince</strong>. He thinks very highly of himself.
<p>How to recognize: Though he might appear to be productive, he does nothing that require actual work.</p>
<p>Pro: He is often dressed nicely. </p>
<p>Con: Can have a bad temper.
</li>
<li><strong>The entrepreneur</strong>. He wants to change the world.
<p>How to recognize: Long-lasting focus on hard problems!</p>
<p>Pro: Can have great results. </p>
<p>Con: Might become a manager.
</li>
</ul>
