<p class="storyHeadSC">A Message from Our Chairman</p>
<div class="imgBlgrSC">
<img src="http://preview.att.net/editorial/images/15/7785315/att_logo.jpg" alt="AT&T News Team" />
</div>
<div class="blgrContentSC"><p class="noMarBot">
</p>
<a href="http://blogs.att.net/consumerblog/blogger/a7785315">AT&T News Team</a> 
<p class="dateCN">September 12, 2013</p></div>
<div class="storyTextCN clr"  id="stryTxt"><P><span style="font-size: small;">We&rsquo;re big believers that social media is a great way to engage with our customers because the conversation is constant, personal and dynamic.</span></p>
<P><span style="font-size: small;"> </span></p>
<P><span style="font-size: small;">Yesterday, we did a post on social media intended to honor those impacted by the events of 9/11. Unfortunately, the image used in the post fell woefully short of honoring the lives lost on that tragic day.</span></p>
<P><span style="font-size: small;"> </span></p>
<P><span style="font-size: small;">I want to personally express to our customers, employees, and all those impacted by the events of 9/11 my heartfelt apologies. I consider that date a solemn occasion each year, a time when I reach out to those I was with on that awful day, share a moment of reflection for the lives lost and express my love of country. It is a day that should never be forgotten and never, ever commercialized. I commit AT&amp;T to this standard as we move forward.</span></p>
<P><span style="font-size: small;"> </span></p>
<P><span style="font-size: small;">--Randall Stephenson, AT&amp;T Chairman and CEO</span></p></div>

{
