<p>Rejoice, muggles: The time has come for J.K. Rowling to Apparate us back into the world of wizards. The author of the <a href="http://mashable.com/category/harry-potter/"><em>Harry Potter</em></a> series is collaborating with Warner Bros. to develop a screenplay based on her novel <em>Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them</em>.</p>
<p><em>Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them</em> was published in 2001 under the pseudonym Newt Scamander. The book was based on a textbook mentioned in the <em>Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone</em>. </p>
<div class="see-also"><p>See also: <a href="http://mashable.com/2013/07/31/harry-potter-new-book-covers/" data-crackerjax="#post-slider">New 'Harry Potter' Book Covers Unveiled</a></p></div>
<p>The new film is neither a prequel nor sequel to the Harry Potter books; it's a standalone "extension of the wizarding world," Rowling said in a press release. </p>
<p>“The laws and customs of the hidden magical society will be familiar to anyone who has read the Harry Potter books or seen the films, but Newt’s story will start in New York, seventy years before Harry’s gets underway," she said in the release.</p>
<p>If we're lucky, maybe we'll get a namedrop of Hogwarts headmaster Albus Dumbledore or a battle between a Hippogriff, Thestral and Basilisk. </p>
<p>Rowling said Warner Bros. approached her about turning the book into a film, but she felt wary of another writing producing it. </p>
<p>"Having lived for so long in my fictional universe, I feel very protective of it and I already knew a lot about Newt," Rowling said. "As hard-core Harry Potter fans will know, I liked him so much that I even married his grandson, Rolf, to one of my favorite characters from the Harry Potter series, Luna Lovegood." </p>
<p>It will be Rowling's screenwriting debut and the first film in a series.</p>
