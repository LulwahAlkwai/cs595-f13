            <h1><a href="http://ericstone.us/?p=1336" rel="bookmark" title="Permanent Link to Evernote to the Rescue">Evernote to the Rescue</a></h1>
        <div class="postdata">
            Posted by ericstone on July 2, 2013 in <a href="http://ericstone.us/?cat=2" title="View all posts in Evernote" rel="category">Evernote</a>, <a href="http://ericstone.us/?cat=5" title="View all posts in Productivity" rel="category">Productivity</a> | <a rel="shortlink" href="http://wp.me/p3eV0f-ly" title="Evernote to the Rescue">&#8734;</a>        </div><!-- .postdata -->
    <p>Wow!! It&#8217;s hard to believe it&#8217;s been over two months since I&#8217;ve last posted on my blog! Time has really flown by!</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>As you may have read in my last post, I moved my office around and did a major purge of &#8220;stuff&#8221;. And the more I purged, the more I have found to purge. And continue to do so!  It baffled my mind why I found a Ziplock bag with 20 telephone patch cords in it. Or why I was saving 25 computer power cords. And why I still had 2 LPT cables when I don&#8217;t even own a computer with an LPT port anymore! And the same with the 3 PS/2 keyboards and mice when none of my computers have anything but USB ports? But what was even more amazing is simply the amount of PAPER that I am finding is not absolutely necessary to save.</p>
<p>Of course, business and tax related documents are stored in a filing cabinet, as well as digitized and stored on 2 different hard drives. But it&#8217;s all of the other paper that I want to save, but not store, that is almost overwhelming. The utility bills, the copies of warranty paperwork, credit card statements, and so on. I used to scan that type of document in, and save as .PDF&#8217;s, but it was becoming quite cumbersome to rename each file from &#8220;SCANxxxx.PDF&#8221; to &#8220;Something  Identifiable.PDF&#8221; That&#8217;s when I decided to move my archive activities into Evernote, and upgrade to a Premium account.</p>
<p>I already had the automatic import folder set up in Evernote, so I created a local notebook and called it &#8220;2013 Archive&#8221;. I then took the last two months of paper, scanned it all in, and by simply using tags, I was able to file all 64 documents in under 30 minutes! A huge time improvement for me! And searching by tags within Evernote for the .PDF, instead of scanning through a list of similarly named files, is so much quicker!.</p>
<p>A huge productivity improvement on all fronts!</p>
