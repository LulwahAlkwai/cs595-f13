<p>The wait is over; the time for rumors is done. On Tuesday at 1 p.m. ET, <a href="http://www.mashable.com/category/apple">Apple</a> will finally reveal its latest iPhones (yes, plural), set iOS 7 free and probably deliver at least one or two surprises. </p>
<p>In the live blog below, <em>Mashable</em> will capture every syllable of CEO <a href="http://mashable.com/category/tim-cook/">Tim Cook’s</a> introduction at Apple’s Cupertino, Calif., headquarters, as well as every detail from what will likely be a parade of execs unveiling the latest Apple gear. </p>
<div class="see-also"><p>See also: <a href="http://mashable.com/2013/09/08/apple-iphone-event-what-to-expect/" data-crackerjax="#post-slider">Apple iPhone Event: What to Expect</a></p></div>
<p>Aside from showing a stunning <a href="http://mashable.com/category/mac-pro/">Mac Pro</a> at its <a href="http://mashable.com/category/wwdc-2013/">Worldwide Developers Conference</a>, Apple has remained fairly quiet on the hardware front. </p>
<p>This is certain to change this week. We expect two new <a href="http://mashable.com/category/iphone/">iPhones</a>, a new Wi-Fi adaptor, an updated MacBook Pro — and who knows what else.</p>
<p>The more intimate "town hall" location on Tuesday signals a smaller launch than, say, last year’s launch of the <a href="http://mashable.com/category/iphone-5/">iPhone 5</a> at the more capacious Yerba Buena Convention Center in downtown San Francisco. The last time <a href="http://mashable.com/category/apple/">Apple</a> held a product event at its headquarters was roughly two years ago when Apple unveiled the <a href="http://mashable.com/category/iphone-4s/">iPhone 4S</a> and <a href="http://mashable.com/category/siri/">Siri</a>. </p>
<p>Will Tuesday’s event include more details on the latest Siri innovation and more Apple Maps mop-up? It's possible. Maybe, just maybe, Tim Cook will bring back “one more thing.” What better way to honor the memory of the late <a href="http://mashable.com/category/steve-jobs/">Steve Jobs</a>, who died two years ago in October?</p>
<p><em>Mashable</em> will have the play-by-play right here, so lock in on this URL. We'll get started on Sept. 10 at 12 p.m. ET.</p>
