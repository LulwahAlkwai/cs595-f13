<section class='article-content'>

<p>The Israeli startup scene needs little introduction. Tel Aviv is rapidly becoming one of the most innovative tech hubs on the planet, vying with London, New York and Berlin as Silicon Valley's second.</p>
<p>Big acquisitions, such as <a href="http://mashable.com/category/waze/">Waze</a> to <a href="http://mashable.com/category/google+/">Google</a> and <a href="http://mashable.com/category/snaptu/">Snaptu</a> to <a href="http://mashable.com/category/facebook/">Facebook</a>, as well a upcoming IPO for Outbrain means Israeli startups are aspiring for big exits. </p>
<div class="see-also"><p>See also: <a href="http://mashable.com/2013/06/27/uk-startups/" data-crackerjax="#post-slider">25 Top UK Startups</a></p></div>
<p>To find out more about the near 5,000 startups in Tel Aviv, Jerusalem and other emerging Israeli hubs, check out <a href="http://mappedinisrael.com/" target="_blank">Mapped in Israel</a>, a definitive, location-based guide. For now, here are our top 20 hottest Israeli startups.</p>
<h2>1. <a href="http://www.gettaxi.co.uk" target="_blank">GetTaxi</a>
</h2>
<p>With the GetTaxi app, people can call taxis with one click. Make a payment, tip and even save a receipt via the app, which also tracks taxi proximity. </p>
