<h1>Computer Science > Computational Complexity</h1>
</div>
<h1 class="title"><span class="descriptor">Title:</span>
Computing Cliques is Intractable</h1>
<div class="authors"><span class="descriptor">Authors:</span>
<a href="/find/cs/1/au:+Fukuyama_J/0/1/0/all/0/1">Junichiro Fukuyama</a></div>
<div class="dateline">(Submitted on 14 May 2013)</div>
<blockquote class="abstract">
<span class="descriptor">Abstract:</span> The class P is in fact a proper sub-class of NP. We explore topological
properties of the Hamming space 2^[n] where [n]={1, 2,..., n}. With the
developed theory, we show: (i) a theorem that is closely related to Erdos and
Rado's sunflower lemma, and claims a stronger statement in most cases, (ii) a
new approach to prove the exponential monotone circuit complexity of the clique
problem, (iii) NC \ne NP through the impossibility of a Boolean circuit with
poly-log depth to compute cliques, based on the construction of (ii), and (iv)
P \ne NP through the exponential circuit complexity of the clique problem,
based on the construction of (iii).
<br />Item (i) leads to the existence of a sunflower with a small core in certain
families of sets, which is not an obvious consequence of the sunflower lemma.
In (iv), we show that any Boolean circuit computing the clique function
CLIQUE_{n,k} (k=n^{1/4}) has a size exponential in n. Thus, we will separate
P/poly from NP also.
<br />Razborov and Rudich showed strong evidence that no natural proof can prove
exponential circuit complexity of a Boolean function. We confirm that the
proofs for (iii) and (iv) are not natural.
</blockquote>
