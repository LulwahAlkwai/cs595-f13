								<h1 class="headline">
									XKCD has some amazing facts about oceans for you								</h1>
								<p class="byline">By <a href="http://grist.org/author/jess-zimmerman/" title="Posts by Jess Zimmerman" rel="author">Jess Zimmerman</a></p>
																							</header>


							<section class="article-body">
								<p><a href="http://xkcd.com/1040/large/"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-91898" title="xkcd_lakes_oceans_detail" src="http://grist.files.wordpress.com/2012/04/screen-shot-2012-04-09-at-2-51-35-pm.png?w=500" alt="" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>Randall Munroe, writer of the web comic XKCD, has put out another one of his dizzyingly meticulous infographics, and this one is about the <a href="http://xkcd.com/1040/">depths of various bodies of water</a>. No, wait, don&#8217;t leave, it&#8217;s actually really cool!<span id="more-91904"></span></p>
<p>Here&#8217;s a teeny version, but you should click to look in more depth (ha) at the whole thing.</p>
<p><a href="http://xkcd.com/1040/large/"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-91897" title="xkcd_bodies_of_water" src="http://grist.files.wordpress.com/2012/04/large.png?w=500" alt="" width="500" /></a></p>
<p>Some of the cool facts contained therein:</p>
<ul>
<li>The <em>Edmund Fitzgerald, Kursk</em>, and <em>Lusitania</em> all sank in water shallower than they were long.</li>
<li>Lake Erie clearly has an inferiority complex.</li>
<li>An emperor penguin can dive deeper than an <em>Ohio</em>-class nuclear sub.</li>
<li>Sperm whales dive crazy crazy deep. Like, the pressure down there is so great, it would push air back in to a ruptured scuba tank.</li>
<li>James Cameron&#8217;s recent <a href="http://grist.org/list/james-cameron-descends-the-mariana-trench/">deep-diving mission</a> went further down in the ocean that the abyss of <em>The Abyss</em>.</li>
</ul>
