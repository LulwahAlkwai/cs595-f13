How Many Government Data Requests Do Major Tech Companies Receive?

   805
   Shares
   [44]Share
   [45]Tweet
   [46]Share
   What's This?
   Data
   [47]Photo
   By Zoe Fox2013-09-10 02:30:54 UTC

   In the first half of 2013, [48]Yahoo received 12,444 government data
   requests, which is more than either [49]Facebook and [50]Twitter did.
   It also received more than [51]Google did in the second half of 2012,
   the last period for which it reported its government requests.

   [52]Statista created this chart comparing the amount of government data
   requests received by four major tech companies. Though Microsoft
   publishes a transparency report, it quantifies its data in 12-month
   periods, so it couldn't be compared with the companies on this chart.

   See also: [53]See How PRISM May Work -- in This Infographic

   This new data was made available by Yahoo, in its [54]first
   transparency report published on Friday, and [55]Facebook, in its
   [56]first transparency report published on Tuesday.

   UPDATE, September 10, 10:45 a.m.: It's important to note that Google
   and Twitter don't publish the number of national-security related
   requests along with the other requests coming from law-enforcement, as
   opposed to the other companies, which publish an aggregate number.
