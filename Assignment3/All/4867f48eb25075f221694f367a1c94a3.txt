					<div class="meta-title">
		<div class="cat-ttl-box" Tablets style="background-color:#9a00b8;"></div><span class="cat-ttl" style="text-transform:uppercase; color:#9a00b8;"><a class="hu" href="http://bgr.com/tablets/">Tablets</a></span>

		<time pubdate class="entry-time" datetime="2013-09-11T10:25:50+00:00">at 10:25 AM</time>
	</div>  <!-- //.meta-title  -->
				<div class="text-column">
										<span class="post-time">10:25 AM</span><p>There really isn&#8217;t much mystery remaining as <a href="http://bgr.com/tag/amazon">Amazon</a> prepares to unveil its second-generation <a href="http://bgr.com/tag/kindle-fire-hd">Kindle Fire HD</a> tablets and its third-generation Kindle Fire slate in the coming weeks. First we published <a href="http://bgr.com/2013/07/09/amazon-kindle-fire-hd-2013-exclusive/">exclusive details about Amazon&#8217;s entire 2013 tablet lineup</a> and then we followed up by revealing <a href="http://bgr.com/2013/07/30/amazon-kindle-fire-hd-2-specs-exclusive/">all of the key specs for the upcoming new Kindle Fire HD tablets</a> as well asÂ <a href="http://bgr.com/2013/08/09/amazon-kindle-fire-3-specs-exclusive/">the new entry-level Kindle Fire</a>. Amazon&#8217;s new high-definition slates are shaping up to be two of the most impressive tablets in the world when they debut, and anticipation is certainly building.Â Now, BGR has obtained exclusive photos of the unannounced next-generation Kindle Fire HD that Amazon is preparing to unveil as soon as later this month, giving the world its first-ever look at Amazon&#8217;s new hardware for 2013.<span id="more-1175532"></span></p>

			<div id='gallery-1176091-1' class='gallery gallery-thumbs gallery-1176091 gallery-single'><div class="gallery-single-thumbs">

<p>We gave an extensive description of the company&#8217;s new tabletsÂ in <a href="http://bgr.com/2013/07/09/amazon-kindle-fire-hd-2013-exclusive/">our first exclusive report covering Amazon&#8217;s tablet plans for 2013</a>, but a picture is worth a thousand words. A trusted source has provided BGR with several photos that reveal <a href="http://bgr.com/tag/kindle-fire-hd2">Amazon&#8217;s next-generation 7-inch Kindle Fire HD tablet</a> for the first time ever. And while only the 7-inch model is pictured in our photo gallery, we have been told that the 8.9-inch model is almost identical to the smaller tablet.</p>
<p>Note that the majority of the back of Amazon&#8217;s next-generation Kindle Fire HD has been obscured in the photos to cover identifiable marks on the device.</p>
<p><img alt="BGR-amazon-kindle-fire-2-5" src="http://boygeniusreport.files.wordpress.com/2013/09/bgr-amazon-kindle-fire-2-5.jpg?w=870&#038;h=576" width="870" height="576" /></p>
<p>As can be seen in our exclusive photos, the 2013 Kindle Fire HD models feature an extensive design overhaul. The rounded shape on the back of the current models has been replaced with a more angular design that gives the device a much harder look. Also as we described, the buttons have been completely redesigned since the current layout was a big pain point for many users.</p>
<p>The stereo speakers on the back of the Kindle Fire HD have now been moved to the top of the device, as seen in several of our exclusive photos. The band across the bottom on the back of the current HD tablets has been removed, leaving a smooth back surface interrupted only by Amazon&#8217;s logo.</p>
<p><img alt="BGR-amazon-kindle-fire-2-1" src="http://boygeniusreport.files.wordpress.com/2013/09/bgr-amazon-kindle-fire-2-1.jpg?w=870&#038;h=557" width="870" height="557" /></p>
<p>For a quick refresher, our sources state that Amazon&#8217;s new 7-inch Kindle Fire HD will feature a high-resolution 1,920 x 1,200-pixel display,Â a quad-core Qualcomm Snapdragon 800 chipset clocked at more than 2GHz, 2GB of RAM, Wi-Fi, optional cellular data, Android 4.2.2 Jelly Bean and either 16GB, 32GB or 64GB of internal storage.</p>
<p>The updated 8.9-inch model is said to feature nearly identical specs beneath itsÂ 2,560 x 1,600-pixel high-definition display, and it also includes an 8-megapixel rear camera.</p>
<p>We&#8217;re told both models are lighter and more comfortable to use than the current-generation tablets they will replace.</p>
<p><img alt="BGR-amazon-kindle-fire-2-6" src="http://boygeniusreport.files.wordpress.com/2013/09/bgr-amazon-kindle-fire-2-6.jpg?w=870&#038;h=549" width="870" height="549" /></p>
<p>Launch details are still a bit fuzzy but we&#8217;re told the 7-inch model may launch in the coming weeks, while the larger 8.9-inch version might not begin shipping until sometime in November. Amazon&#8217;s 2012 Kindle Fire HD launches were staggered as well; theÂ Kindle Fire HD 7&#8243; was released in mid-September last year while theÂ Kindle Fire HD 8.9&#8243; wasn&#8217;t available until two months later in mid-November.</p>
<p><img alt="BGR-amazon-kindle-fire-2-3" src="http://boygeniusreport.files.wordpress.com/2013/09/bgr-amazon-kindle-fire-2-3.jpg?w=870&#038;h=576" width="870" height="576" /></p>
