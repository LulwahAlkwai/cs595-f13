<section class='article-content'>

<p><a href="http://mashable.com/category/twitter/">Twitter</a> has filed initial paperwork to go public, the company revealed on Thursday. The company tweeted its announcement on Thursday afternoon:</p>
<div class="see-also"><p>See also: <a href="http://mashable.com/2013/09/12/twitter-ipo-analysis/" data-crackerjax="#post-slider">Twitter's IPO Filing: What It Means</a></p></div>
<blockquote class="twitter-tweet" data-partner="tweetdeck">
<p>Weâ€™ve confidentially submitted an S-1 to the SEC for a planned IPO. This Tweet does not constitute an offer of any securities for sale.</p>
<p>â€” Twitter (@twitter) <a href="https://twitter.com/twitter/statuses/378261932148416512" target="_blank">September 12, 2013</a></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Shortly after, Twitter added: </p>
<blockquote class="twitter-tweet">
<p>Now, back to work. <a href="http://t.co/e4lK8e7pY9" target="_blank">pic.twitter.com/e4lK8e7pY9</a></p>
<p>â€” Twitter (@twitter) <a href="https://twitter.com/twitter/statuses/378262115431092224" target="_blank">September 12, 2013</a></p>
</blockquote>
<p>Twitter is currently valued at around <a href="http://mashable.com/2013/01/03/twitter-11-billion/" data-crackerjax="#post-slider">$11 billion</a>, according to estimates. Twitter is able to file confidentially thanks to the JOBS [Jumpstart Our Business Startups] Act, which lets companies go public without public scrutiny. JOBS applies to companies with less than $1 billion in revenues.</p>
