<p>Pandora released a new iOS 7 version of its iPhone app and a compleately redesigned iPad app Wednesday.</p>
<p>“We’ve taken all of the things that we’ve learned about the lean-in experience about Pandora on the web and our phone applications, and brought that the tablet with a new user experience built from the ground up around the larger screen environment of the tablet,” Pandora CTO Tom Conrad told Mashable.</p>
<p>Optimized for larger screens, the app makes it easy to add variety to a station with artist suggestion, helps you explore more about an artist or album on screen while you listen, and provides a detailed timeline customized for each individual listener. Social sharing also comes to the iPad for the first time with the release, giving users the opportunity to publish their music activity to Facebook, or share links to their favorite stations on Twitter.</p>

<p>From Evernote to Shazam, it’s a big day for <a href="http://mashable.com/category/apps/">app</a> updates.</p>
<p>iOS 7 is <a href="http://mashable.com/2013/09/18/ios-7-now-available/" data-crackerjax="#post-slider">now available for download</a>, and many of the App Store’s biggest players updated their apps accordingly.</p>

The OS, which is available for the <a href="http://www.mashable.com/iphone">iPhone</a>, <a href="http://www.mashable.com/ipad">iPad</a> and iPod touch, brings a new look and brighter colors to the devices.</p>
