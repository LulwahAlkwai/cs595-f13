For me, this winter break would not be festive without an <span style="background-color: lime;"><span style="color: red;"><b>End-of-Year</b></span><span style="color: red;"><b> Attempt-At-Humor </b></span><span style="color: red;"><b><span style="color: red;"><b>Academic Writing </b></span>Contest Of Some Sort</b></span></span>. Oh sure, I can get in the holiday mood by walking into any store, cafe, or gas station and being subjected to a bewildering variety of renditions of the most appalling Christmas songs possible, I can (try to) put reindeer antlers on my most docile cat, and I can even decorate cookies in vile colors (Fig. 1),<br />
<br />
<a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-HZliiMWLGZQ/UNC-5EZRp6I/AAAAAAAAAVc/n060XQh9YGY/s1600/vileXcookie.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="173" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-HZliiMWLGZQ/UNC-5EZRp6I/AAAAAAAAAVc/n060XQh9YGY/s200/vileXcookie.jpg" width="200" /></a>but it just wouldn't be the same without an <span style="background-color: red;"><span style="color: lime;"><b>End-of-Year Attempt-At-Humor Academic Writing Contest of Some Sort</b>. </span></span><br />
<br />
To recap the last 4 contests:<br />
<ul style="text-align: left;">
<li>2008: <a href="http://science-professor.blogspot.com/2008/12/sop-contest-entry-1.html">The Statement of Purpose</a>;</li>
<li>2009: <a href="http://science-professor.blogspot.com/2009/12/letter-of-reference-contest-results.html">Letters of Reference;</a> </li>
<li>2010: <a href="http://science-professor.blogspot.com/2010/12/hey-professor-i-am-honestly-truly-sorry.html">Why I Missed the Final Exam</a>; and</li>
<li>2011: <a href="http://science-professor.blogspot.com/2012/01/cover-letter-contest-vote.html">The Cover Letter</a> for academic positions.</li>
</ul>
<br />
What now? Announcing: <span style="background-color: white;"><b>the CV</b></span> (curriculum vitae/resume).<br />
<br />
I know what you are (possibly) thinking: <b>the CV?</b> That is not <i>writing</i>. <br />
<br />
And so I reply, if you are (possibly) thinking that: <i>au contraire</i>. I have learned in the past few months that even the smallest, shortest, fragmentary attempt to convey information in a visual way is "writing". I learned this in some "meetings".<br />
<br />
But that's not why I have selected the CV as this year's writing theme. I selected the CV because I have been continually amazed over the years by the fact that it is possible to go so far astray with what is seemingly a simple document in which some biographical and other facts are arranged to describe a person's qualifications for a position. Even more amazing to me is the realization that it is possible for someone to create (what seems to me to be) an <b>obnoxious CV</b>; not in the <i>nature </i>of the facts but in <i>how</i> they are presented. <br />
<br />
Readers who wish to participate: Your challenge is to create an <u>entirely fictitious</u> or at least <u>heavily disguised</u> CV that fulfills one or more of the following outcomes, <br />
<ul style="text-align: left;">
<li>entertainment;</li>
<li>horror;</li>
<li>mentoring;</li>
<li>all of the above.</li>
</ul>
whilst not veering (too far) from the norms of the academic CV. In addition, it would be great if the fabricated CV is not too long.<br />
<br />
I hasten to emphasize that submitted CVs should not humiliate any actual persons other than yourself. The purpose of this contest is to have fun, relieve end-of-term stress, and perhaps make a dramatic and useful point or three about potential CV pitfalls.<br />
<br />
As always, parody -- subtle or savage -- is encouraged, although I realize (from e-mails I have received over the years) that these writing contests may generate some anxiety in those who are in the process of 
creating the very document that is being featured. I have therefore added "mentoring" as a possible outcome, even if the mentoring is done in an ungentle way. <br />
<br />
Entries can be sent to femalescienceprofessor@gmail.com (do not send attachments) and will be 
reviewed by the FSP Editorial Board. I will be traveling in an unusual place throughout most of 
late December - early January, but I will post selected entries as 
internet access permits.<br />
<br />
Entries will be accepted until the position is filled. Review of entries will begin on or soon after I start receiving them in the next week or two. Eventually there may be a vote on the Most Entertaining, Most Horrifying, and/or Most Useful Fake CV. <br />
<br />
<br /></div>
