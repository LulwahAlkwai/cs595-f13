<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
Simons Institute opening, with pictures. 
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-9174750624108937137'>
Today was the official kickoff for the Simons Institute, as well as the start of the two special programs in big data and real analysis. For the last few days I've been busy getting my paperwork organized over at Calvin Hall, which is a beautifully redone circular building named after the Nobel Laureate and Berkeley professor (more on the building <a href="http://simons.berkeley.edu/about/calvin-lab">here</a>).<br />
<br />
The inside is very conducive to collaborative work. The offices are organized around the periphery of the building, and are functional, but not overly so. The idea is that people will congregate in the large interior open spaces that are covered with whiteboards and comfy chairs.<br />
<br />
This is what the interior looks like: (apologies for the picture quality)<br />

The next plan is to pave the <b>outer</b> walls of the building at ground level with chalkboards, so people can think and chat outside as well as inside. Ah, Berkeley weather. I'm told the boards are already there, and just need to be installed, so I hope to have pictures soon.&nbsp;</div>

<br /></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
There are 93 visitors between the two programs (53/39 by my rough count) which includes 22 Fellows. Offices are either two or three-person, and there are some large cubicle spaces with 7+ inhabitants. The visitors are all on the second and third floors of the building, which both have large open areas (the pictures above are from the second floor).&nbsp;</div>
