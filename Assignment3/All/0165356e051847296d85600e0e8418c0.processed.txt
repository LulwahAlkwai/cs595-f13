AT&T Apologizes for 9/11 Tribute That Many Found Offensive

   September 11, 2013 at 10:31 am PT
     * [59]Tweet
     * [60]Share on Facebook
     *
     *
     * Share
     * [61]Print

   [62]Screen Shot 2013-09-11 at 10.04.45 AM AT&T posted an apology
   Wednesday after a 9/11-related tribute that many found to be in poor
   taste.

   The Twitter post said "Never Forget" and featured a photo of the New
   York skyline with a smartphone showing the area where the Twin Towers
   stood. A [63]post on BuzzFeed called it "without a doubt the tackiest
   9/11 memorial on the Internet."

   "We apologize to anyone who felt our post was in poor taste," AT&T said
   after the backlash. "The image was solely meant to pay respect to those
   affected by the 9/11 tragedy."

   The Los Angeles Lakers also [64]posted a 9/11 tweet of dubious taste
   (since deleted).
