<section class='article-content'>

<p>AMC delivered its third major announcement within a week on Tuesday, informing <em>Mad Men</em> fans that season 7 will be split into two seven-episode parts titled "The Beginning" and "The End of an Era." The revelation comes on the heels of AMC revealing a <a href="http://mashable.com/2013/09/11/amc-breaking-bad-spinoff-saul/" data-crackerjax="#post-slider">spinoff series</a> for <em>Breaking Bad</em> and a <a href="http://mashable.com/2013/09/16/walking-dead-spinoff-series-amc/" data-crackerjax="#post-slider">companion series</a> to <em>The Walking Dead</em>.</p>
<p>The series' final 14 episodes will be split over two consecutive springs in 2014 and 2015, akin to the model <a href="http://mashable.com/category/amc/">AMC</a> used for <em>Breaking Bad</em>. Past seasons included only 13 episodes.</p>
<div class="see-also"><p>See also: <a href="http://mashable.com/2012/03/22/mad-men-tech/" data-crackerjax="#post-slider">Mad Men Tech: 9 Devices That Changed the 1960s Office</a></p></div>
<p>"<span class="microcontent" data-fragment="we-plan-to-take" data-description="We plan to take advantage of this chance to have a more elaborate story told in two parts" data-micro="1">We plan to take advantage of this chance to have a more elaborate story told in two parts</span>, which can resonate a little bit longer in the minds of our audience," Matthew Weiner, <em>Mad Men</em> creator and executive producer, said in a statement. "The writers, cast and other artists welcome this unique manner of ending this unique experience." </p>
<p>AMC President Charlie Collier notes the approach helped <a href="http://mashable.com/category/breaking-bad/"><em>Breaking Bad</em></a> "nearly double the number of viewers to its second half premiere than had watched any previous episode."  </p>
<p>"In an era where high-end content is savored and analyzed, and catch-up time is used well to drive back to live events, we believe this is the best way to release the now 14 episodes than remain of this iconic series," Collier added.</p>
<p>Previously, HBO split the final season of <em>The Sopranos</em> for which Weiner was a writer.</p>
<p>Do you think this season-splitting model will enhance the final season of <em>Mad Men</em>? Chime in with your thoughts in the comments, below.</p>
