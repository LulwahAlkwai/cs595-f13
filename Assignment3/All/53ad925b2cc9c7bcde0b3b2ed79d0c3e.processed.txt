Expanded 'Mad Men' Season 7 to Air Over 2 Years

   1.1k
   Shares

   [45]Tweet

   Mad-men-season-seven
   [47]Bah-sq
   By Brian Anthony Hernandez2013-09-17 20:32:24 UTC

   AMC delivered its third major announcement within a week on Tuesday,
   informing Mad Men fans that season 7 will be split into two
   seven-episode parts titled "The Beginning" and "The End of an Era." The
   revelation comes on the heels of AMC revealing a [48]spinoff series for
   Breaking Bad and a [49]companion series to The Walking Dead.

   The series' final 14 episodes will be split over two consecutive
   springs in 2014 and 2015, akin to the model [50]AMC used for Breaking
   Bad. Past seasons included only 13 episodes.

   See also: [51]Mad Men Tech: 9 Devices That Changed the 1960s Office

   "We plan to take advantage of this chance to have a more elaborate
   story told in two parts, which can resonate a little bit longer in the
   minds of our audience," Matthew Weiner, Mad Men creator and executive
   producer, said in a statement. "The writers, cast and other artists
   welcome this unique manner of ending this unique experience."

   AMC President Charlie Collier notes the approach helped [52]Breaking
   Bad "nearly double the number of viewers to its second half premiere
   than had watched any previous episode."

   "In an era where high-end content is savored and analyzed, and catch-up
   time is used well to drive back to live events, we believe this is the
   best way to release the now 14 episodes than remain of this iconic
   series," Collier added.

   Previously, HBO split the final season of The Sopranos for which Weiner
   was a writer.

   Do you think this season-splitting model will enhance the final season
   of Mad Men? Chime in with your thoughts in the comments, below.
