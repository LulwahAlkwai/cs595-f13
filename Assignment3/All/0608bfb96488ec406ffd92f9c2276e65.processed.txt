   The wait is over; the time for rumors is done. On Tuesday at 1 p.m. ET,
   [48]Apple will finally reveal its latest iPhones (yes, plural), set iOS
   7 free and probably deliver at least one or two surprises.

   In the live blog below, Mashable will capture every syllable of CEO
   [49]Tim Cook's introduction at Apple's Cupertino, Calif., headquarters,
   as well as every detail from what will likely be a parade of execs
   unveiling the latest Apple gear.

   See also: [50]Apple iPhone Event: What to Expect

   Aside from showing a stunning [51]Mac Pro at its [52]Worldwide
   Developers Conference, Apple has remained fairly quiet on the hardware
   front.

   This is certain to change this week. We expect two new [53]iPhones, a
   new Wi-Fi adaptor, an updated MacBook Pro -- and who knows what else.

   The more intimate "town hall" location on Tuesday signals a smaller
   launch than, say, last year's launch of the [54]iPhone 5 at the more
   capacious Yerba Buena Convention Center in downtown San Francisco. The
   last time [55]Apple held a product event at its headquarters was
   roughly two years ago when Apple unveiled the [56]iPhone 4S and
   [57]Siri.

   Will Tuesday's event include more details on the latest Siri innovation
   and more Apple Maps mop-up? It's possible. Maybe, just maybe, Tim Cook
   will bring back "one more thing." What better way to honor the memory
   of the late [58]Steve Jobs, who died two years ago in October?

   Mashable will have the play-by-play right here, so lock in on this URL.
   We'll get started on Sept. 10 at 12 p.m. ET.
