<p>Obsessed with Internet <a href="http://mashable.com/category/porn/">porn</a> on his laptop and smartphone, Joseph Gordon-Levitt's character in bawdy <a href="http://mashable.com/category/comedy/">comedy</a> <em>Don Jon</em> lives in a fantasy world. In it, Jon Martello's weekly real-life sex romps pale in comparison to the web smut he watches while fervently masturbating. </p>
<p>"Real life is actually, I would say, so much more beautiful and so much more rich and nuanced than anything could ever be on a two-dimensional screen, and you'll miss it if you're too busy comparing your real life to these fantasies," Gordon-Levitt told <em>Mashable</em>, explaining what he hopes moviegoers will grasp from <a href="http://donjonmovie.tumblr.com" target="_blank"><em>Don Jon</em></a>. </p>
<p><img src="http://rack.3.mshcdn.com/media/ZgkyMDEzLzA5LzE0LzZiL0RvbkpvbkdJRi44NGQ2Ni5naWYKcAl0aHVtYgkxMjAweDk2MDA-/cb7bd45e/e4f/Don-Jon-GIF.gif" alt="Don Jon GIF" class="fullwidth" data-width="480"><small><em><a href="http://donjonmovie.tumblr.com" target="_blank">DonJonMovie.tumblr.com</a></em></small></p>
<p>Gordon-Levitt is making his directorial debut with <em>Don Jon</em> after working under Steven Spielberg in <em>Lincoln</em> and Christopher Nolan in <em>The Dark Knight Rises</em>. He wrote the film and cast an all-star lineup that includes Scarlett Johansson, Julianne Moore and Tony Danza.</p>
<p>"Joseph is remarkably assured — I think he could have whatever kind of directing career he wants," Moore told <em>Mashable</em> on the red carpet at the New York City premiere this week. "He really knows what he wants, he has a vision and he knows how to accomplish it."</p>
<p>Moore plays Esther, a classmate who tries to help Jon solve his intimacy issues, while Johansson portrays love interest Barbara Sugarman. Danza, who also starred with Gordon-Levitt in 1994's <em>Angels in the Outfield</em>, plays Jon's potty-mouthed father.</p>

<p>Nowadays, a <a href="http://mashable.com/category/movie/">movie</a> without a social marketing blitz is like a car with missing wheels, so Gordon-Levitt and film distributor Relativity Media have brought <em>Don Jon</em> online through a Jon Martello <a href="http://mashable.com/category/twitter/">Twitter</a> account (managed by Gordon-Levitt) as well as a <a href="http://donjonmovie.tumblr.com" target="_blank">GIF-driven Tumblr</a>. </p>
<p>Gordon-Levitt writes the tweets for <a href="https://twitter.com/DonJon" target="_blank">@DonJon</a>, infusing Jon's persona into the text. </p>
<p>"It's really fun, man, it's like a continuation of the writing process and I love that because normally you make something, you act as the character, you finish the movie and that's it," Gordon-Levitt said on the red carpet. </p>
<p>"This has allowed the character to live on in a certain way," he added, "although what I'm writing is more of a prequel because, if you see the movie, he begins to evolve a bit and break out of his mold, but the stuff I've been writing on Twitter as him, well, he has not evolved."</p>

<p>Aside from making the rounds for <em>Don Jon</em>, Gordon-Levitt is busy operating his collaborative production company <a href="http://www.hitrecord.org/" target="_blank">hitRECord.com</a> for short films, records, books and now a TV show.</p>
<p>"It's called <em>hitRECord on TV</em> and it's the biggest production we've tackled," he said. "We're making it right now so any artist can contribute to it, whether you're a writer, musician, actor, illustrator and animator, and I'm directing all of these different projects and, as always, we pay the people whose work ends up in the final product."</p>
<p>The television series on Pivot will incorporate performances, short films and conversations. </p>
