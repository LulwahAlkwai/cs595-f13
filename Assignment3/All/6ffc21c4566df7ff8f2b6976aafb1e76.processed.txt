BlackBerry Z30 Has a 5-Inch Display and BB OS 10.2

   817
   Shares
   [44]Share
   [45]Tweet
   [46]Share
   What's This?
   [47]Stan-schroeder
   By Stan Schroeder2013-09-18 11:04:25 UTC

   IFRAME: [48]https://www.youtube.com/embed/cQVKISYQ31s

   [49]BlackBerry has launched the Z30, a 5-inch smartphone, the first one
   to sport the new version of BlackBerry OS, 10.2.

   Following in the footsteps of the Z10, the Z30 doesn't have a physical
   keyboard, once a staple of BlackBerry's high-end models. Instead, a
   1,280x720 pixel, 5-inch Super AMOLED display covers most of its face.

   Also on board are a dual-core 1.7 GHz processor with a quad-core Adreno
   320 GPU, 2GB of RAM, stereo sound, a new-generation antenna that
   "dynamically tunes reception to give you better connectivity in low
   signal areas" and a 2880mAh battery.

   Equally important as the hardware is the phone's BlackBerry OS 10.2
   software which brings the Priority Hub -- a single place to manage all
   your conversations and notifications -- as well as a refined BBM
   messaging experience, which now works regardless of what app you're in.

   See also: [50]Who Would Buy BlackBerry?

   BlackBerry Z30 will become available with select carriers and retailers
   around the world beginning next week in the UK and Middle East. All
   other regions can expect to see the device in the holiday season.
   Pricing announcements have been left to individual carriers and
   retailers.

   And for owners of older BlackBerry models -- namely, the Z10, Q10 and
   Q5 -- you can expect to get the 10.2 version of the OS mid-October.
